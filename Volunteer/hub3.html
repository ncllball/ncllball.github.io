Umpiring: The voice of calm between the lines

Umpires are the immediate guardians of pace, safety, and sportsmanship. We pair newer volunteers with veterans on lower diamonds so you can develop your strike zone and mechanics in real time. Pre-season clinics cover field positioning, conflict de-escalation, and how to communicate with coaches the Little League way.

What an assignment looks like:

  • Arrive 20 minutes early for a plate meeting and field check.
  • Run a Majors double-header: plate for Game 1, swap to bases for Game 2.
  • Debrief with the UIC afterward so you keep sharpening skills.

Every accurate call gives kids confidence that their effort matters.

Team-Level Leaders: The daily pulse of the league

This spoke is perfect if you love being around your player's team each week. We provide coaching clinics, practice plans, and administrative templates so you can focus on relationships. Team managers often onboard new families, while assistant coaches run stations so players move constantly.

What a season looks like:

  • Pre-season: attend coaching orientation, align on team values, set expectations with parents.
  • In-season: run two practices plus game-day warmups, keep snack and dugout rotations shared in TeamSnap.
  • Post-season: celebrate awards night, gather feedback for next year.

The trio keeps the team steady so kids can simply show up ready to learn.

League-Level Roles: Strategic and steady

The league spoke stretches beyond individual teams to coordinate facilities, finance, and communication. Board members meet monthly (more often pre-season) to keep policy aligned with Little League International. Key volunteers plug into shorter sprints: concessions leads train teen workers, sponsorship chairs build partnerships, and communications volunteers translate schedules into parent-friendly updates.

What stewardship feels like:

  • A Safety Officer audits equipment and AED stations before Opening Day.
  • A Concessions Director orders inventory weekly and mentors youth workers.
  • A Communications lead recaps board decisions as Friday e-mails to 800 families.

These leaders ensure every spoke is aligned with the mission.

Ad Hoc Squads: Fast help when it matters

Our Event List and Ground Gremlins are text-first volunteer pools. You opt in once, then receive short messages any time we need bodies. No guilt if you cannot make it—just jump in when the timing works.

Event List example: We host a Hit-A-Thon at Lower Ross. Volunteers cover registration tables, run music, and tally pledges for two hours.

Ground Gremlins example: After a downpour, we call for six adults to broom puddles, nail drag infields, and reset mound clay. Ninety minutes later, the fields are playable.

Small bursts of effort keep the entire wheel balanced for the weekend.
