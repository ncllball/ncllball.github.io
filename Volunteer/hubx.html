Volunteer Hub

Welcome to the new Volunteer Hub. This is the one place to raise your hand for the roles and quick-call crews that keep NCLL running.

Start with core needs: coach, assistant coach, and team manager. If you have more to give-or just an extra hour here and there-join our opt-in lists for fast callouts by email or text.

No Experience? No Problem! No experience is required to become a volunteer. Most of the volunteer opportunities require little or no training. NCLL will provide you with any necessary training (as well as support and encouragement) to help you succeed. There are four primary Volunteer Roles at NCLL:

  • Game-day and event helpers (photo day, HR Derby, meetings)
  • Field crew on wet or windy days
  • On-call umpires and scorekeeping support
  • Gear moves, setup/teardown, and odd jobs
  • Special skills (photography, graphics, carpentry, tech)

Anyone can help-parents, grandparents, siblings, neighbors, alumni, and community partners. Pick what fits your time; if a callout does not work, no worries, maybe next time.

We've all got our place, come and find yours!

Team Level Volunteers

Team-level volunteers work directly with players throughout the season. These roles are the heart of our league, providing coaching, mentorship, and organization that helps kids develop skills, confidence, and a love for the game.

Why Volunteer at the Team Level?

  • Watch your child (or others) grow in skills and confidence
  • Build lasting friendships with families
  • Make a direct, visible impact on kids' experiences
  • Learn alongside the players
  • Create memories that last a lifetime

Explore specific team roles: Head Coach, Assistant Coach, or Team Manager to understand expectations, time commitment, and training for each role.

Head Coach

  • Leads the team, makes key decisions, recruits staff, plans practices, assigns positions, enforces rules, and fosters a love of baseball.
  • Responsible for practices, game strategy, and player development.
  • Plans and runs practices with fast-paced drills tailored to your roster.
  • Manages games: set lineups, make in-game moves, communicate with umps.
  • Develops players through consistent feedback and encouragement.
  • Shapes culture: keep the dugout positive, inclusive, and focused on growth.
  • Coordinates volunteers: align assistant coaches and team managers.
  • Time commitment: 6-8 hrs/week (two practices, games, planning).
  • Training: Pre-season clinics, practice plans, and support from division coordinators.

Assistant Coach

  • The backbone of our league, assisting with younger players, running drills, and helping the Head Coach focus on individual instruction. If you can follow directions, you can assist!
  • Right-hand energy - supports the head coach and works directly with players during practices and games.
  • Run practice stations and small-group instruction.
  • Coach during games - base coaching, dugout management, situational tips.
  • Focus on skills such as hitting, pitching, or catching.
  • Step up when needed if the head coach is unavailable.
  • Build relationships with players and parents.
  • Time commitment: 4-6 hrs/week (practices + games).
  • Perfect for: newer coaches, former players, or anyone who enjoys teaching fundamentals.

Team Manager

  • Often considered the "Team Mom" or "Team Dad."
  • Helping with pitch count and/or GameChanger scorekeeping.
  • Assisting in the dugout.
  • Distributing uniforms.
  • Collecting paperwork.
  • Coordinating the end-of-season party.
  • Logistics quarterback: handles communication and logistics so coaches can focus on the field.
  • Communication hub: send reminders, coordinate schedules, manage the team list.
  • Organize logistics: snacks, photos, team parties, dugout prep.
  • Record keeping: scorebooks, results, volunteer hours.
  • Liaison: relay info between families and division coordinators.
  • Support fundraising or special events when needed.
  • Time commitment: 3-5 hrs/week (games plus admin work).
  • Perfect for: organized parents, logistics lovers, or anyone wanting to help off the field.

League Level

League Level Volunteers League-level volunteers keep NCLL running smoothly behind the scenes. These roles require a larger time commitment but offer the opportunity to shape league-wide programs, policies, and culture for all 800 families.

Board Members

The NCLL Board oversees operations and votes on key positions annually. Many volunteers start by running a program or initiative before joining the Board. League leadership roles are elected positions that guide the strategic direction of NCLL, with terms typically 1-2 years and elections held annually.

Key Positions Include:

  • President - Overall league leadership, community relations, strategic planning
  • Vice Presidents (Baseball/Softball) - Oversee all baseball or softball operations, division coordination, coach support
  • Secretary - Meeting minutes, records management, official correspondence
  • Treasurer - Budget management, financial reporting, bookkeeping
  • Player Agent - Registration oversight, roster management, player eligibility
  • Safety Officer - Risk management, injury protocols, facility inspections
  • Information Officer (CIO) - Website, communications, technology systems
  • Time Commitment: 8-15 hours per week during season; 3-5 hours off-season

Key Volunteers (Non-Board)

Key Volunteers: These volunteers identify a need, passion, or problem and help the Board find solutions. Tasks range from organizing Photo Day to managing the website.

Essential roles that support specific league functions. These positions report to board members but don't require board election.

Key Positions Include:

  • Division Coordinators - Support coaches in a specific division (AAA, Majors, etc.), organize assessments, handle scheduling
  • Umpire Coordinator - Recruit, train, and schedule umpires for all games
  • Equipment Manager - Inventory, maintenance, and distribution of league equipment
  • Field Coordinator - Liaise with Seattle Parks, schedule field usage, coordinate maintenance
  • Sponsorship Coordinator - Solicit sponsors, manage relationships, coordinate banner placements
  • Volunteer Coordinator - Recruit volunteers, match people to roles, track volunteer hours
  • Concessions Manager - Organize snack shack operations, manage inventory and volunteers
  • Tournament Director - Plan and execute special events (opening day, all-stars, home run derby)
  • Time Commitment: Varies by role; typically 4-8 hours per week during season

Skills Needed

  • Organization and attention to detail
  • Communication and people skills
  • Problem-solving and flexibility
  • Commitment to the league's mission
  • Willingness to learn (we provide training!)

Why Volunteer at the League Level?

  • Shape the experience for 800+ families
  • Build leadership and management skills
  • Deep involvement in your community
  • Network with passionate, dedicated volunteers
  • Leave a lasting legacy for future players

Umpiring

Often called the “third team on the field,” the umpiring “Junta”—as they like to be called—keep games moving, fair, and safe.

NCLL boasts one of the best umpire crews in the district. While many believe umpires are part of Little League, they actually operate independently and work with us, not for us. By joining the Umpire Registry, umpires gain access to a unique network dedicated to improving the game for players everywhere.

Overview Umpires are the backbone of every game, ensuring fair play and keeping the action moving. Whether you're brand new to the role or have years of experience, we provide training, support, and mentorship to help you succeed behind the plate and on the bases.

What Umpires Do

  • Call balls and strikes - make judgment calls on pitches to keep the game fair
  • Rule on plays - determine safe/out calls at bases and in the field
  • Maintain game flow - keep the game moving at an appropriate pace
  • Ensure player safety - watch for rule violations that could lead to injuries
  • Communicate with coaches - explain calls clearly and manage coach interactions professionally

Time Commitment

  • Games typically last 1.5-2 hours
  • Most umpires work 1-3 games per week during the season (April-June)
  • We're flexible and work around your schedule

Training & Support

  • Pre-season training clinics (in-person and online)
  • Mentorship from experienced umpires
  • Rules clinics and ongoing development
  • Protective equipment provided by the league

Why Volunteer as an Umpire?

  • Best seat in the house for every game
  • Learn the game inside and out
  • Earn stipends for your time (optional)
  • Build confidence and decision-making skills
  • Join a supportive community of fellow umpires

Umpire Roles

  1. Umpire Coordinator — schedule & support. The coordinator makes sure every game has coverage and that volunteers know where to be.
    • Build weekly assignments and handle last-minute swaps
    • Track availability, certifications, and stipend preferences
    • Communicate rainouts, time changes, and field moves
    • Celebrate milestones—first solo plate job, perfect attendance, etc.
    • Time commitment: 3–4 hrs/week plus heavier planning time pre-season
  2. Umpire in Chief — lead the crew. The UIC mentors the staff, keeps mechanics sharp, and settles tricky rules questions.
    • Run preseason mechanics and rules clinics
    • Shadow new umpires on the diamond and provide real-time coaching
    • Standardize interpretations so every field calls the same game
    • Coordinate evaluations and feedback with the board
    • Time commitment: 4–6 hrs/week
  3. On-Field Umpire — call the game. Plate and base umpires keep every matchup fair, safe, and on time.
    • Rotate between plate and base assignments
    • Manage game tempo, enforce rules, and communicate with coaches
    • Apply training from clinics and peer mentoring
    • Model calm, confident decision-making for players and parents
    • Time commitment: 1–3 games per week (90 minutes each) during the season

Ready to jump in? Visit NCLL’s Reach Out page for umpires to see our training calendar, gear support, mentorship details, and how to get on the “Junta” registry for assignments.

Ad-Hoc Volunteering For those who can't commit to a season-long role, there are plenty of one-time or short-term opportunities to pitch in. From field maintenance to event support, every bit of help makes a difference!

Pop-Up Support
Overview
Can't commit to a season-long role? No problem! Pop-Up Support volunteers are the flexible heroes who jump in when needed. Whether you have a free Saturday or want to help with a specific project, there's always a place for you.

Event Volunteers
Special events - help create memorable moments for players and families throughout the season.

Event Opportunities Include:

Opening Day - Field setup, registration table, parade coordination, ceremony support (April)
Photo Day - Guide teams to photographer, organize lineups, distribute order forms (May)
Home Run Derby - Pitch to contestants, track scores, manage brackets (May/June)
All-Star Events - Tournament support, concessions, scorekeeping (June/July)
Skills Clinics - Station support, equipment management, registration (Various dates)
Fundraisers - Raffle sales, auction organization, event setup (Throughout season)
End-of-Season Celebrations - Party planning, award distribution, cleanup (June)
Time Commitment: 2-4 hours per event; volunteer for one event or many

Sign-up: We send email blasts before each event with volunteer needs and sign-up links

Ground Gremlins
Field maintenance crew - keep our playing surfaces safe, beautiful, and game-ready.

What Ground Gremlins Do:

Field prep - Dragging infields, lining basepaths, setting bases before games
Mound maintenance - Repairing pitcher's mounds, filling holes, packing clay
General upkeep - Weeding, raking, cleaning dugouts, emptying trash
Equipment care - Organizing storage sheds, maintaining field equipment
Safety checks - Identifying hazards, reporting issues to field coordinator
Seasonal projects - Spring field prep, end-of-season deep cleaning
Time Commitment: Flexible! Join scheduled work parties (usually Saturday mornings) or grab a rake whenever you're at the field

Work Parties: Typically 2-3 hours; held monthly during season and for pre-season prep

Drop-in help: Even 15 minutes of raking between games makes a difference

Perfect For
Parents with unpredictable schedules
People who want to help without a long-term commitment
Families looking for a fun group activity
Anyone who wants to give back in small, meaningful ways
Volunteers who want to try different roles to find their fit

"Super Volunteers" (Employee Matching)

Employer Matching Gifts. Did you know that many companies match their employees' volunteer hours with cash grants to the nonprofits they volunteer with? These corporate philanthropy programs--often called Volunteer Match or Time Matching--can significantly benefit our league. Historically, companies like Microsoft, Boeing, Starbucks and more have well-thought-out programs to support their employees giving back to their communities. It's not just Fortune 500 companies, though, that support their employees' communities; smaller companies have programs as well. See if your company offers this kind of program.

Action item for volunteers: Ask your HR department if your company has a program to donate dollars for your volunteer time. Each company has its own process.

Volunteer time already means the world to NCLL--we literally could not operate without volunteers. With these company-giving programs, volunteers can easily support what they care about even more. Time literally is money. Funds from volunteer giving programs help pay for uniforms, player development, equipment and more.
