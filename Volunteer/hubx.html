Volunteer Hub

Welcome to the new Volunteer Hub. This is the one place to raise your hand for the roles and quick-call crews that keep NCLL running.

No Experience? No Problem! No experience is required to become a volunteer. Most of the volunteer opportunities require little or no training. Head Coach, Assistant Coach, and Team Manager keep the roster running smoothly and we're always in need.

Team Level Volunteers

Team-level volunteers work directly with players. These roles are the heart of our league, providing coaching, mentorship, and organization that helps kids develop skills, confidence, and a love for the game.

Why Volunteer at the Team Level?

  • Watch your child (or others) grow in skills and confidence
  • Build lasting friendships with families

Why Volunteer at the Team Level? (Continued)

  • Make a direct, visible impact on kids' experiences
  • Create memories that last a lifetime

Explore specific team roles: Head Coach, Assistant Coach, or Team Manager to understand expectations, time commitment, and training for each role.

Head Coach

  • Leads the team, makes key decisions, recruits staff, plans practices, assigns positions, enforces rules, and fosters a love of baseball.
  • Responsible for practices, game strategy, and player development.
  • Plans and runs practices with fast-paced drills tailored to your roster.
  • Manages games: set lineups, make in-game moves, communicate with umps.

Head Coach (Continued)

  • Develops players through consistent feedback and encouragement.
  • Shapes culture: keep the dugout positive, inclusive, and focused on growth.
  • Coordinates volunteers: align assistant coaches and team managers.
  • Time commitment: 6-8 hrs/week (two practices, games, planning).
  • Training: Pre-season clinics, practice plans, and support from division coordinators.

Assistant Coach

  • Backbone role: run drills and keep the Head Coach free for instruction.
  • Right hand during practices and games—hands-on with players.
  • Lead stations and small-group reps.
  • Coach games: bases, dugout flow, quick situational tips.

Assistant Coach (continued)

  • Focus on a skill lane: hitting, pitching, catching—pick your strength.
  • Step in when the head coach is out.
  • Build trust with players and parents.
  • Time: 4-6 hrs/week (practices + games).
  • Great for newer coaches, former players, or anyone who loves fundamentals.

Team Manager

  • Team Mom/Dad energy—keeps off-field pieces moving.
  • Scorekeeping backup: pitch count or GameChanger.
  • Dugout support during games.
  • Uniforms and paperwork wrangling.
  • Coordinate the end-of-season party.
  • Communication hub: reminders, schedules, team list.

Team Manager (continued)

  • Logistics: snacks, photos, team parties, dugout prep.
  • Records: scorebooks, results, volunteer hours.
  • Liaison between families and division coordinators.
  • Pitch in on fundraising or special events.
  • Time: 3-5 hrs/week (games + admin).
  • Great for organized parents or logistics lovers.

League Level

League Level Volunteers keep NCLL running smoothly behind the scenes. Board and Key Volunteer roles take a bigger time commitment but let you shape programs, policies, and culture for all 800 families.

If you have a passion to grow—or a pain point to fix—tell us and we'll connect you to the role where you can make it happen.

Board Members

The NCLL Board oversees operations and votes on key positions annually. Many volunteers start by running a program or initiative before joining the Board. League leadership roles are elected positions that guide the strategic direction of NCLL, with terms typically 1-2 years and elections held annually.

Key Board Positions Include:

  • President - Sets vision, leads relations, strategic planning
  • VPs (Baseball/Softball) - Operations, divisions, coach support
  • Secretary - Minutes, records
  • Treasurer - Budget, reporting, books
  • Registrar - Registration flow, payments, forms, roster cleanup
  • Player Agent - Rosters, eligibility
  • Safety - Risk mgmnt, safety protocols
  • Information (CIO) - Website, communications, tech systems
  • Time Commitment: 8-15 hours per week in season; 3-5 off-season

Key Volunteers (Non-Board)

Key Volunteers identify a need, passion, or problem and help the Board find solutions. Tasks range from organizing Photo Day to managing the website. These roles support specific league functions, report to board members, and don't require election.

Key Positions Include:

  • Division Coordinators - Support coaches in one division, run assessments, keep schedules on track
  • Umpire Coordinator - Recruit, train, and schedule umpires
  • Equipment/Field Coordinator - Manage gear inventory, field usage, and maintenance
  • Sponsorship Coordinator - Bring in sponsors, manage relationships and banners
  • Volunteer Coordinator - Recruit volunteers, match people to roles, track hours
  • Time Commitment: Varies by role; typically 4-8 hours per week

Skills Needed

  • Organization and attention to detail
  • Communication and people skills
  • Problem-solving and flexibility
  • Commitment to the league's mission
  • Willingness to learn (we provide training!)

Why Volunteer at the League Level?

  • Shape the experience for 800+ families
  • Build leadership and management skills
  • Deep involvement in your community
  • Network with passionate, dedicated volunteers
  • Leave a lasting legacy for future players

Umpiring

Often called the "third team on the field," the umpiring Junta—as they like to be called—keep games moving, fair, and safe.

Overview

NCLL boasts one of the best crews in the district. Umpires operate independently alongside us; the Umpire Registry connects them to a network focused on improving the game everywhere.

They anchor every game—keeping it fair and moving. New or experienced, you get training, support, and mentorship to succeed behind the plate and on the bases.

What Umpires Do

  • Call balls and strikes to keep the game fair
  • Rule on plays at bases and in the field
  • Maintain game flow and pace
  • Watch for safety issues and rule violations
  • Communicate calls and manage coach interactions

Time Commitment

  • Games typically last 1.5-2 hours
  • Most umpires work 1-3 games per week during the season (April-June)
  • Flexible scheduling to fit your week

Training & Support

  • Pre-season clinics (in-person and online)
  • Mentorship from experienced umpires
  • Rules clinics and ongoing development
  • Protective gear provided by the league

Why Volunteer as an Umpire?

  • Best seat in the house for every game
  • Learn the game inside and out
  • Optional stipends for your time
  • Build confidence and decision-making skills
  • Join a supportive community of fellow umpires

Umpire Roles

Umpire Coordinator
  • Schedule coverage and manage swaps
  • Track availability, certifications, preferences
  • Communicate rainouts and time changes
  • Celebrate milestones (first plate job, perfect attendance)
  • Time commitment: 3-4 hrs/week plus heavier pre-season planning
Umpire in Chief
  • Mentor the crew; keep mechanics sharp
  • Run preseason mechanics and rules clinics
  • Shadow new umpires and give real-time coaching
  • Standardize interpretations across fields
  • Time commitment: 4-6 hrs/week
On-Field Umpire
  • Rotate between plate and base assignments
  • Manage game tempo, enforce rules, communicate with coaches
  • Apply clinic training and peer mentoring
  • Model calm, confident decision-making
  • Time commitment: 1-3 games per week (about 90 minutes each)

Ready to jump in? Visit NCLL's Reach Out page for umpires to see our training calendar, gear support, mentorship details, and how to get on the "Junta" registry for assignments.

"POP UP" Support

It's baseball, and just like on the field, "Pop-Ups" happen?we don't always know when or where. Pop-Up Support volunteers are the flexible heroes who jump in for single-shift, ad hoc needs. Can't commit to a season-long role? No problem. From field maintenance to event support, every one-off helps, and it's commitment-free when you join our volunteer lists page.

Ad Hoc Heroes & Ground Gremlins

Ad Hoc Heroes come together and keep special events moving to create memorable moments for players and families all season.

Event Opportunities Include:

  • Opening Day - Field setup, registration table, parade coordination, ceremony support (April)
  • Photo Day - Guide teams to photographer, organize lineups, distribute order forms (May)
  • Home Run Derby - Pitch to contestants, track scores, manage brackets (May/June)
  • All-Star Events - Tournament support, concessions, scorekeeping (June/July)
  • Skills Clinics - Station support, equipment management, registration (Various dates)
  • Fundraisers - Raffle sales, auction organization, event setup (Throughout season)
  • End-of-Season Celebrations - Party planning, award distribution, cleanup (June)
  • Time Commitment: 2-4 hours per event

Sign-up: We send email blasts before each event with volunteer needs and sign-up links

Ground Gremlins are the field-first crew-keeping every diamond safe, beautiful, and game-ready.

What Ground Gremlins Do:

  • Field prep - Dragging infields, lining basepaths, setting bases before games
  • Mound maintenance - Repairing pitcher's mounds, filling holes, packing clay
  • General upkeep - Weeding, raking, cleaning dugouts, emptying trash
  • Equipment care - Organizing storage sheds, maintaining field equipment
  • Safety checks - Identifying hazards, reporting issues to field coordinator
  • Seasonal projects - Spring field prep, end-of-season deep cleaning
  • Time Commitment: Flexible! Join scheduled work parties (usually Saturday mornings) or grab a rake whenever you're at the field

Work Parties: Typically 2-3 hours; held monthly during season and for pre-season prep

Drop-in help: Even 15 minutes of raking between games makes a difference

"Super Volunteers" (Employee Matching)

Employer Matching Gifts. Did you know that many companies match their employees' volunteer hours with cash grants to the nonprofits they volunteer with? These corporate philanthropy programs--often called Volunteer Match or Time Matching--can significantly benefit our league. Historically, companies like Microsoft, Boeing, Starbucks and more have well-thought-out programs to support their employees giving back to their communities. It's not just Fortune 500 companies, though, that support their employees' communities; smaller companies have programs as well. See if your company offers this kind of program.

Action item for volunteers: Ask your HR department if your company has a program to donate dollars for your volunteer time. Each company has its own process.

Volunteer time already means the world to NCLL--we literally could not operate without volunteers. With these company-giving programs, volunteers can easily support what they care about even more. Time literally is money. Funds from volunteer giving programs help pay for uniforms, player development, equipment and more.
