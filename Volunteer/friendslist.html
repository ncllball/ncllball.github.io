Volunteer Team Lists

New: Volunteer Lite (2025)

Our core volunteer needs remain--these are ADDIITONAL volunteer opportunities: coach, assistant coach, and team manager.

Volunteer Lite is a new way to help out—in addition to our regular roles. These lists are for folks who want to pitch in for specific events, with no long-term commitment or baseball experience required. We’ll reach out by email or text when help is needed—if you’re available, great! If not, maybe next time.

  • Home Run Derby helpers
  • Photo Day assistants
  • Field work on tough weather days
  • Annual coaches meeting setup/cleanup
  • Special talents (e.g. photography)

Anyone can volunteer—parents, grandparents, siblings, aunts, uncles, retirees, community leaders, former ball players, friends, neighbors, and more. All parents of NCLL players are strongly encouraged to volunteer. It’s a great way to spend quality time with your child and support our community.

Pick one or more opt-in lists to get quick callouts when help is needed. These are lightweight, action-first groups you can join or leave any time. Team Parents: consider nominating one or two families from your team to help keep fields playable and events running smoothly

Opt-in lists

Ground Gremlins

  • Purpose: Rapid-response field work when weather hits
  • What you’ll do: Rake, broom, sponge, lay turface, clear drains, help make fields playable
  • Callouts: SMS-first text list for urgent needs; occasional email for weekend planning
  • Best for: Parents near our parks or anyone who can jump in on short notice
  • Frequency: Only when needed (rain days)

On-Call Umpires

  • Purpose: Backup umpire coverage when a crew is short
  • What you’ll do: Step in for base or plate as needed; training and gear guidance available
  • Callouts: SMS for same-day needs; email for upcoming games
  • Best for: Volunteers comfortable on the field or willing to learn
  • Frequency: As-needed

Event Day Crew

  • Purpose: Keep Opening Day, Photo Day, and Home Run Derby running smoothly
  • What you’ll do: Set up, check-in, line management, breakdown
  • Callouts: Email-first with shift signups; occasional SMS reminders
  • Best for: Parents who like organized, time-boxed tasks
  • Frequency: A few events per season

Gear & Equipment Movers

  • Purpose: Help move and stage equipment when seasons or programs change
  • What you’ll do: Move L-screens, tees, pitching machines, bases, mounds, storage organization
  • Callouts: Email with planned windows; occasional SMS for quick helpers
  • Best for: Anyone willing to lift and carry with a buddy
  • Frequency: Infrequent, scheduled windows

iPhone numbers are used only for urgent volunteer callouts. You can opt out any time; reply STOP to a text or email the organizer
