Overview

NCLL refreshed its visual identity for 2025 to create a unified mark that represents both softball and baseball, modernizes presentation, and remains compliant with Little League branding rules. Below is a concise record of how the final logo was developed and approved.

Timeline

  1. Initiation: Need identified for an inclusive, contemporary mark; Chris Cunningham volunteered to coordinate.
  2. Collaboration: Partnered with an NCLL parent designer; feedback gathered from board members (Nicole, Ryan, Kirsten, Cole, others).
  3. Constraints: Aligned to Little League requirements (no depiction of a ball; clarity at small sizes; multi-format versatility).
  4. Concept Presentation (Nov 19): Three directions shared: “Field of Dreams,” “Sandlot,” “League of Our Own.”
  5. Direction Chosen: “Field of Dreams” advanced; discussion covered accent color balance, neutral gender representation, and scalability.
  6. Refinement: Two iterative review cycles produced cleaned geometry, simplified line weight, and standardized color palette (core red / deep navy / white).
  7. Final Approval (Dec 17): Board vote confirmed adoption of the final badge and supporting lockups.

What Changed

  • Unified clover + letterform system for digital, apparel, and print
  • Simplified color set for consistent reproduction
  • Improved legibility at favicon / social avatar scale
  • Clear hierarchy: League Badge (formal), Primary Mark (NC Clover), Secondary / Flat variants (utility)

Summary

The process balanced creativity, compliance, and broad representation. The adopted system supports flexible future use while preserving a recognizable NCLL identity.
