Step 1: Determine Eligibility for NCLL

  • NCLL is in Washington's District 8 and covers Wallingford, Greenlake, Fremont, Phinney Ridge, Greenwood, and Ballard
  • Players must live within NCLL boundaries or attend an eligible school
  • New players outside boundaries need a waiver to play; for that and all waiver matters, please contact NCLL's president

Step 2: Determine Your Player's Division

League Age

  • League Age : Your player's age on August 31st (Baseball) or December 31st (Softball) determines their league age for that season
  • League Age is not the same as chronological age (the age they are on their birthday)
  • Baseball: If a player will be 9 years old on August 31, 2027, they are considered "league age 9" for the 2027 season
  • Softball: If a player will be 9 years old on December 31, 2026, they are considered "league age 9" for the 2027 season (Softball uses different dates)
  • Use the age charts and calculator in Step 2(a) below to determine league age

Assessments

  • Required for all AAA, Majors, or Junior division players, for both softball and baseball, regardless of previous division
  • Not tryouts—all players will be placed on a team. Used for skill evaluation and competitive balance
  • The Player Agent will conduct the assessments and make recommendations based on the players' skills and abilities
  • Families will be notified of assessment dates, times, and locations via email

Team Assignments

  • Assignments finalized by late February
  • Playing up: Requests to play in higher divisions reviewed based on safety, coach feedback, and division balance

Baseball Notes (Click to expand)

Note: All kids play T-ball. Baseball is open to boys and girls. Softball divisions are gender-specific and are for girls only, starting from A Division.

1 T-ball (5-6) is for 5's and 6's; 6's with a year of T-ball should consider KCP or Baseball A.

2 Kindergarten Coach Pitch (KCP, 6-7) moves at a faster pace than T-ball — live coach pitching with the tee available as a fallback. League age 6 required per Little League rules. Played on the T-ball field.

3 Baseball A (7-8) uses a coach-pitch and machine pitch blend with hardball and is for 7's/8's, but 6's with a year of T-ball are encouraged to register.

4 Baseball AA (8-9) is mainly for 8's/9's, though skilled 7-year-olds occasionally qualify.

5 Baseball AAA (9-11) is the primary division for 10's and 11's — though skilled 11's may be placed in Majors instead. 9's fill remaining roster spots based on assessment scores, with eligibility finalized in late January (see Assessments for details). Highly-skilled 8's are placed here on rare occasions.

6 Baseball Majors (10-12) is mainly for 11's and 12's, with a few highly-skilled 10's.

7 Baseball Juniors (13-14) is for 13's & 14's. While 12's can join, it's not advised as they'd forego a year of Majors.

8 Baseball Seniors (15-16) is mainly for 15's & 16's, though as young as 13's are eligible.

9 Juniors & Seniors play on 90ft fields when available. These fields have limited availability and different scheduling constraints than standard fields — days of the week and start times may vary more than other divisions.

10 Days of week reflect recent seasons and the district-level cadence coordinated with other District 8 leagues. Final schedules are confirmed after registration when field allocations and team counts are set, and may shift due to availability, weather, and league-wide constraints; these days are planning guidance rather than a guarantee.
