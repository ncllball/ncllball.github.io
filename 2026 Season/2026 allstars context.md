All-Star Team Formation
Upon completion of the tryout, a group of NCLL coaches will collectively determine the players that make each team's roster.
We expect more players at each age level to be interested in playing All-Stars, than we have roster spots available. Each age level will have capable players that do not make the All-Star team.
Teams will be formed based on:
Overall player performance & skills (incl. practices, games, and assessments)
Will take into account regular season league (MAJ, AAA->MAJ, AAA)
Player character, based on coaches’ feedback and observations
Attitude towards teammates, opponents, umpires, fans, and coaches
Work ethic, practice habits, coachability 
Positional need & versatility

IMPORTANT INFORMATION FOR PARENTS
All-Stars teams play in district tournaments against other Little Leagues in the same age division. It is a lot of fun, players get as much, if not more, baseball instruction as they did in the regular season, and there is no additional registration fee required for your child to participate.
With exceptions for players participating in high school baseball and those with approved Medical Waivers, players must have played at least 60% of the season on a regular North Central Little League team in order to play All-Stars. Players must meet all eligibility criteria established by Little League Baseball.  NCLL is required to confirm eligibility by obtaining residence eligibility and proof of age documentation from each player.  It is absolutely critical that NCLL receives this from each player or the team could be disqualified. 

NCLL is allowed only one All-Stars team in each of the divisions that tournaments are offered.  Our league’s priority is to put a competitive team on the field in each division. Since there are normally more applicants than spots, it may be necessary to cut some players.
The commitment for All-Stars is more intense than the regular season. Depending on how well each team does, it could go until late July, or early August if they win District and advance to the state tournament and beyond. The players will be required to be at most practices and ALL games. Normally teams practice or scrimmage 4-6 days a week with up to 2 warm-up tournaments before the district tournament starts.
If my child participates in warm-up tournaments, it will be at their own risk and families cannot hold Little League, NCLL, coaches, and/or any volunteers liable for injuries and/or damages that arise from participation.
The NCLL philosophy is that competitive performance and a positive experience must coexist. I understand that "experience" does not guarantee equal playing time.
The 24-Hour Rule: Parents agree to wait 24 hours (or until the next day) after any game or practice before contacting a coach with a grievance or feedback regarding playing time or strategy. These conversations will be respectful and handled in private. Coaches are volunteers approved by the Board of Directors and should be treated with respect at all times.
Failure to adhere to the 24-hour policy or engaging in disrespectful behavior toward coaches, officials, or other families may result in:
Physical removal from the premises.
Suspension from attending future NCLL All-Star events
Review of family’s future eligibility within the league
For 2026, District 8 Tournaments start between June 18 and July 6.  District 8 Tournaments
typically include 11 or 12 teams (5 or 6 teams for Juniors) and last 9-11 days (or 5-6 days for Juniors).  The winner of the District 8 Tournament goes to the State Tournament.  The winning team at State (12 and above only) may advance to Regionals and from Regionals to Nationals or the Little League World Series, which ends in late August.  There are no “set” dates or times for the games, as each tournament is double elimination.  Whether you win or lose determines your next game time.  There will be travel involved within or out of state.  While the league sets aside limited funds to help defer the cost, there may be some out-of-pocket expenses, as well as the possibility of a fundraiser if additional money is needed.
