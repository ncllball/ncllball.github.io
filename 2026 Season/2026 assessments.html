2026 Assessments (AAA & Majors)

Mandatory assessments are how we build balanced teams and place every player where they will grow. Please register your player and, if possible, grab a volunteer slot to keep check-in, warm-ups, and station timing moving.

Skilled 9's are eligible and welcome to come out. Space in AAA for 9's is subject to roster availability after 10's and 11's are placed + an assessment score that puts them into the draftable AAA pool. The size of that pool will be determined by how many 10's, 11's, and total teams we have. If you have registered for AA, that has absolutely NO bearing on Assessment day. 9's should sign up for AAA spots.

New to NCLL or AAA? Read Assessments: What to Expect for the basics of what happens at this yearly NCLL event. You can also learn about the AAA draft in our AAA Draft Overview.

  • Location: Robert Eagle Staff MS
  • Roles needed: check-in/welcome (bibs, first aid, directing players, setup), drill coaches (grounders, timing runs), assessors (prioritize Majors/AAA coaches), tents/awnings setup (rain or shine)

Reminders

  • What to bring: Glove, water, warm clothing; turf shoes/sneakers/cleats (no metal spikes); helmet (loaners may be available); bat optional.
  • Weather: Baseball is rain or shine; softball is indoors. Watch email and the site morning-of for updates.
  • Softball footwear: Softball is indoor at Wallingford BGC - wear indoor/clean street shoes (no metal spikes).
