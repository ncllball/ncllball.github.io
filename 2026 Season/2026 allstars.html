General All-Stars Information (Applies to Both Baseball & Softball)

North Central Little League (NCLL) proudly sponsors All-Star teams to represent our league in the Little League District 8 Tournament. This is an exciting opportunity for dedicated players to showcase their skills, compete at a high level, and potentially advance to state or even international tournaments.

Being part of an All-Stars team is a unique and rewarding experience. Don't miss this chance to be part of something special! We plan to field three All-Star baseball teams and four All-Star softball teams this season. We may also field an Intermediates (50/70) baseball team, as we did last year, pending a coach and sufficient player interest.

Eligibility Requirements (All Players)

  • Residency/School Enrollment: Must meet Little League requirements. Waivers need prior year establishment.
  • Proof of Age: Original birth certificate required for District 8
  • Minimum Regular Season Game Participation: Players must have played at least 60% of the regular season on an NCLL team (6 games minimum for Seniors). High school baseball players and those with approved Medical Waivers are exceptions.
  • Player Availability: Availability for the District Tournament is a requirement to make the team — see the tournament schedule below for dates

General Commitment

  • Time: Practices start June 1. Required attendance at most practices and ALL games.
  • Baseball: Up to 5 events/week
  • Softball: 5–6 events/week
  • Some teams may participate in warm-up tournaments
  • Tournament Path: District 8 (double-elimination, local, late June–mid July) → State Competitions → Regionals/Little League World Series (12U Majors winners only in 2026)
  • Cost: No registration fee for All-Stars. Uniform fee will be implemented (scholarship/financial aid available). Separate fees may apply for warm-up tournaments. Winning teams traveling to Regional tournaments will receive additional fundraising support.

Family & Player Support (General)

  • Fundraising participation is expected from players and families
  • Parents can help make the team a fun experience by helping to plan for social activities, car decorating parties, etc.
  • Some of our teams will also help staff All-Star games by running the scoreboard, selling raffle tickets, and providing refreshments for our tireless umpires
  • 24-Hour Rule: Parents agree to wait 24 hours after any game or practice before contacting a coach with a grievance or feedback about playing time or strategy. These conversations will be respectful and handled in private.
  • Please be ready to help out!

Tryout Details

  • Date: Friday, May 15th
  • Time: 6:00 PM – 9:00 PM (10U @ 6:00–7:00 PM | 11U @ 7:00–8:00 PM | 12U @ 8:00–9:00 PM)
  • Location: Lower Woodland Field #3 or #4
  • Attendance: Encouraged, but not required
  • Format: Similar to the January Assessments, with drills evaluated by NCLL coaches

Selection Criteria

  • Players' attitude towards teammates, opponents, umpires, fans, and coaches
  • Players' work ethic, practice habits, and coachability based on coaches' feedback and observations
  • Overall player performance (incl. practices, games, and tryout)
  • Regular season division context (Majors, AAA→Majors, AAA)
  • Baseball skills, as determined by tryout evaluation and feedback from coaches
  • Positional need and versatility
  • Player availability for the majority of All-Star practices, scrimmages, and warm-up tournaments
  • Player availability for the District Tournament is a requirement to make the team

Team Formation

  • We expect more players at each age level to be interested in playing All-Stars than we have roster spots available
  • Each age level will have players that do not make the All-Star team
  • Coaches have been evaluating players since January, and will continue to do so until teams are formed
  • Parents should expect rosters to be announced the afternoon of Friday, May 22nd
  • Upon completion of the tryout, a group of NCLL coaches will collectively determine the players that make each team's roster

Commitment

  • NCLL All-Star teams will have a practice, scrimmage, or tournament the majority of weeknights and weekends from June 1st through the end of their season
  • An All-Star player will be expected to attend the majority of scheduled events with their All-Star Team, AND ALL OFFICIAL ALL-STAR GAMES
