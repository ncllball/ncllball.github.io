Mandatory Rest Thresholds

Rest is determined by pitches thrown in a day. Crossing a threshold increases required calendar rest days before the pitcher may pitch again. A pitcher removed before reaching the next threshold keeps the lower rest requirement. If a pitcher reaches 20 pitches during an at‑bat and is removed before the batter completes the at‑bat, the threshold used is based on the final count at batter completion.

*Examples assume no weather postponements or double‑header special cases; calendar day = full midnight-to-midnight day.

  • Catcher to pitcher and pitcher to catcher eligibility restrictions still apply (see division pages).
  • If a pitcher reaches a higher threshold while finishing a batter, use the higher rest category.
  • Scorekeepers should reconcile pitch counts each half‑inning (or minimally each full inning) between teams.

Catcher / Pitcher Eligibility (Battery Restrictions)

The following Little League Baseball Regulation VI provisions govern when a player may move between pitching and catching within the same day. These rules exist to reduce acute fatigue and overuse risk for the throwing arm.

Battery Movement Restrictions
Direction Condition Trigger Eligibility Result Key Nuance
Pitcher → Catcher Pitches ≤ 40 (removed before delivering pitch #41 to a new batter) May catch later same game If pitcher reaches 40 during an at‑bat and is removed before next batter, still eligible
Pitcher → Catcher Pitches ≥ 41 Ineligible to catch rest of day Completion of batter where pitch #41 occurred is allowed; ineligibility set once batter completed
Catcher → Pitcher Caught 3 or fewer innings May pitch (subject to pitch count & rest) Partial innings count if any defensive play while in catching position
Catcher → Pitcher Caught 4 or more innings Ineligible to pitch that day Warm‑up or bullpen only does not count; inning counts once a pitch is received in live play

Always verify with the current season Green Book in case of annual rule adjustments. Keep a parallel catcher innings & pitch log for clarity.

  • Inning counts for a catcher are based on any defensive pitches received in that half inning.
  • A player who starts an inning as catcher and is moved mid‑inning has that inning count.
  • Maintain real‑time visibility: whiteboard, shared sheet, or scorebook annotation.

Safety & Best Practices

  • Warm‑up progression: dynamic movement → band work (if available) → flat toss → bullpen
  • Remove pitchers showing mechanical breakdown or abrupt velocity drop—protect long term arm health
  • Discourage breaking ball focus (especially pre‑Majors); prioritize command & change of speed
  • Track cumulative weekly pitches (not just per day) to avoid stacked threshold days
  • Communicate with families about external pitching (select / lessons) to avoid hidden overuse
