Find the right division for your player

North Central Little League offers baseball divisions for players ages 5-16, from first-time players learning the basics to competitive teams preparing for high school ball. Each division is designed to match players' developmental stages with appropriate skill challenges and game formats.

How to choose: Divisions are primarily based on your child's league age (their age on August 31st). Hover over any division card below to see full details about practice schedules, field locations, and what makes each division unique. If you're unsure which division is right, contact our registrar for guidance.

Registration for all divisions opens in early January. Assessments for AAA and Majors divisions typically occur in late February.

Note for coaches: If you need more detailed information than what's provided here - such as specific rules, draft procedures, or coaching resources - you're probably a coach (or possibly future volunteer)! Head over to our Coaches Hub for comprehensive guides, training materials, and division-specific coaching information.

Tee Ball

Start here for first swings and throws in a fun, confidence-building setup. The focus is on movement and smiles.

Tee Ball is designed for boys and girls league age 5 or 6, typically in Kindergarten or their last year of pre-school.

  • League ages: 5-6
  • Format: Safety ball; tee swings every inning
  • Weekly events: Pre Tue/Thu practices; season Tue practice & Sat game
  • Fields: Upper Ross Park

Introduction to Hitting and Fielding

Tee Ball provides young players with their first exposure to batting and playing defense in a relaxed setting.

  • Safety ball; everyone bats off the tee each inning
  • Players rotate across positions throughout the season
  • Coaches emphasize movement, confidence, and smiles

Team Selection Criteria

We place players to keep friends together while balancing teams.

  • Coach and teammate requests considered at placement
  • Siblings and school friends grouped when possible
  • All-girl team option available upon request

Practice and Game Schedule

Expect a steady rhythm that builds routine for new families.

  • Preseason: two practices each week
  • In-season: one weeknight practice + Saturday game
  • Sessions run 60-75 minutes with high movement

Practice Location & Next Steps

Teams typically gather at Upper Ross Park with coaches providing equipment and practice plans.

Kindy Coach Pitch

Kindergarten Coach Pitch, or "Kindy Coach Pitch", is a new division that we have piloted for two years. This division allows league age 6 kids an opportunity to play a slightly more challenging game with a moving ball and actual outs, but is also adaptive for kids at varying skill levels. Parents considering signing up their player for this division should consider whether the kid has the coordination, attention span for hit balls, and emotional maturity to handle outs.

  • League ages: 6 (after Tee Ball or coach OK)
  • Format: Coach pitch; safety ball with tee backup
  • Weekly events: Pre Tue/Thu practices; season Wed practice & Sat game
  • Fields: Upper Ross Park or Upper Woodland

1. Introduction to Hitting and Fielding

  • Hit a live pitch while keeping a tee ready for confidence
  • Safety ball keeps first outs low stress
  • Rules adjust through spring to match player readiness

2. Team Selection Criteria

  • Coach and teammate requests logged at signup
  • Siblings and classmates grouped when possible
  • Prior Tee Ball season or coach approval required

3. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Preseason: two practices weekly
  • Regular season: one weeknight practice + Saturday game

4. Practice Location

  • Upper Ross Park or Upper Woodland

A - Coach Pitch

The A Division is primarily composed of 7 and 8-year-olds (league age) and serves as the next step for players graduating from Tee Ball or Kindy Coach Pitch.

  • League ages: 7-8
  • Format: Coach pitch; hardball; continuous lineup
  • Weekly events: Pre Tue/Thu practices; season Thu practice & Sat game
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland

1. Introduction to Coach Pitch

  • Coach pitch with a hardball builds live-pitch timing
  • Stations reinforce core throwing, fielding, and base skills
  • Continuous lineup keeps every hitter in the action

2. Team Selection Criteria

  • Coach and teammate requests logged at registration
  • Siblings and classmates grouped whenever possible
  • League balances rosters so games stay competitive

3. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Preseason: two practices every week
  • Season: one weeknight practice + Saturday game

4. Practice Locations

  • BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

AA - Coach/Player Pitch

The AA Division is primarily composed of 8 and 9-year-olds (league age) and serves as the next step for players graduating from A baseball. It's the last division where players are not divided by skill level through assessments.

  • League ages: 8-9 (top 7s may move up)
  • Format: Coach/player pitch blend; min-play rules
  • Weekly events: Pre Tue/Thu practices; season Wed practice & Sat game
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland

1. Player Age

  • Primarily 8-9 year olds; top 7s join by coach invite
  • Players build on A Division fundamentals with faster tempo

2. Game Format

  • Coach-pitch early; player-pitch phases in by midseason
  • Reinforces rules, decision-making, and game awareness
  • Minimum play rules keep innings and at-bats balanced

3. Team Selection Criteria

  • Coach and teammate requests gathered at signup
  • Siblings and schoolmates grouped when possible
  • League balances rosters to maintain parity

4. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Preseason: two practices every week
  • Season: one weeknight practice + Saturday game

5. Practice Locations

  • BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

AAA

AAA Baseball is home base for league-age 10–11 players—plus a few assessed 9s—who are ready for full player-pitch tempo and broader defensive roles.

Focus: translate AA fundamentals into game-speed execution with consistent reps on the mound, in the field, and at the plate.

  • League ages: 10–11 (select 9s)
  • Format: Player pitch; pitch-count & min-play rules
  • Weekly events: Pre Mon/Wed/Fri practices; season Tue practice + Thu/Sat games
  • Fields: BF Day & Lower Woodland
Register Coaching info

Skill Progression

Coaches layer movement prep, throwing progressions, and defensive stations so players lock in mechanics before the reps speed up.

  • Movement prep + live throwing progressions keep timing sharp.
  • Pitching/catching rotations follow clear workload plans.
  • Situational scrimmages reinforce cut-offs, reads, communication.

Assessments & Team Formation

Preseason evaluations keep rosters balanced so every matchup stays competitive and every player sees meaningful roles.

  • Assessments + coach feedback drive a parity-focused draft.
  • Attend evaluations (or makeup) to stay AAA eligible.
  • Managers pre-plan pitching/catcher workloads for safe reps.

Season Rhythm & Facilities

Families can count on a steady cadence: structured preseason practices, game weeks with built-in recovery, and fields equipped for quick transitions.

  • Preseason: 3 practices/week. In-season: 1 practice + midweek & Sat games.
  • BF Day & Lower Woodland provide lights, bullpens, cages, warm-up space.
  • Weekly coach email covers focus areas, gear, volunteers, weather pivots.

Majors

The Majors Division is composed of all 12-year-olds, along with the better 11-year-old players, and occasionally a few 10-year-olds. It serves as the next step for players in this age group, especially those who excelled on previous AAA teams.

  • League ages: 12s + top 11s (select 10s)
  • Format: Player pitch; LL rules + Local D8
  • Weekly events: Tue practice; Thu & Sat games
  • Fields: BF Day or The Cloverleaf

1. Player Evaluations

  • All 10s/11s attend tryouts to be draft eligible
  • Every 12-year-old participates to finalize rosters

2. Instructional and Competitive

  • Blends instruction with higher stakes competition
  • Focus on game-speed reps while refining fundamentals
  • Minimum play rules keep innings balanced

3. Manager Focus

  • Managers pursue competitive play plus positive culture
  • Players chase consistent mechanics and baseball IQ

4. Team Formation

  • Draft uses current tryout data and prior coach reports
  • Parity across rosters keeps the schedule competitive
  • Tryout attendance (or approved makeup) required

5. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Preseason: three practices weekly
  • In-season: up to three games each week

6. Practice Locations

  • BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

Junior Division

The Junior Division is specifically designed for players league age 13 and 14.

  • League ages: 13-14
  • Format: 90' bases; D8 Schedule; drafted teams
  • Weekly events: Tue/Thu games; Sun practice
  • Fields: Greenlake or Lower Woodland #1

1. Field Size

  • Full-size 90' diamond mirrors high school play

2. Minimum Play Requirements

  • Minimum play rules ensure every roster spot sees action

3. Competition and District Play

  • Competes across District 8 (Ballard, QA, Magnolia, Richmond, NW & NE Seattle)
  • Regular season sets seeds for All-Star tournament play

4. Tryouts and Team Formation

  • Tryouts slot players; every athlete lands on a roster
  • Snake draft aligns pitching depth and key positions
  • Parity remains the priority for regular-season play

5. Practice Locations

  • Greenlake or Lower Woodland Field #1
