Welcome to the Seniors Division of our little league baseball program! This division is specifically designed for players league age 15 and 16. At this level, players experience baseball that closely mirrors high school play while maintaining the core values and safety standards of Little League.

Seniors will follow the green book rules set forth by Little League International. The following is provided as clarification or exceptions.

  • 2024 Junior Baseball Rules (context for feeder division) (PDF): Junior Baseball Rules
  • 2024 D8 Supplemental Junior Baseball Rules (PDF): Supplemental Junior Rules

Field Specifications

  • Games are played on a regulation 90-foot diamond
  • Pitcher's mound is 60 feet, 6 inches from home plate
  • Games are typically played at Lower Woodland Field #1

Competition Format

  • Teams compete in District 8 interleague play
  • Games are scheduled against teams from:
    • Ballard
    • Queen Anne
    • Magnolia
    • Richmond
    • Northwest Seattle
    • Northeast Seattle

Team Formation

  • Players must attend tryouts for team placement
  • Teams are formed through a draft process
  • Balance of skill levels is considered when forming teams
  • All registered players will be placed on a team

Game Rules

Time Limits

  • Games are 7 innings
  • No new inning after 2 hours and 30 minutes
  • 10-run mercy rule applies after 5 innings

Minimum Play Requirements

  • Every player must play at least 6 defensive outs
  • Every player must have at least one at-bat
  • Free substitution is allowed except for pitchers

Pitching Rules

  • Pitchers are limited to 95 pitches per day
  • Required rest days based on pitch count must be observed
  • Balks are called and enforced
  • Catcher ↔ Pitcher crossover: Battery Eligibility Table
  • April / May: follow Early Season Ramp guidance when building stamina after off‑season

Equipment Requirements

  • BBCOR -3 bats only
  • Metal cleats are permitted
  • Full catching equipment required, including protective cup
  • Double-ear flap batting helmets required

Final Thoughts

The Seniors Division offers players the opportunity to compete at a high level while preparing for high school baseball. Our focus remains on player development, sportsmanship, and creating a positive baseball experience for all participants.
