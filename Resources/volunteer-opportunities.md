NCLL is run entirely by volunteers — from the board of directors to the parent who helps herd cats at a Tee Ball practice. We need you.

## What Our Volunteers Do

- Register and assign **600+ kids**, 100+ coaches and managers, and dozens of umpires
- Schedule **60+ teams**, 500 games, and thousands of practice/training sessions
- Maintain and distribute **600+ jerseys** (in numerous sizes), thousands of baseballs/softballs, and safety equipment
- Coordinate, allocate, and help maintain a limited set of play fields
- Support managers and coaches with snacks, pictures, Mariners Day, and the Jamboree

## Open Positions

### Division Coordinator — Tee-Ball
Supports team managers in logistics and operations throughout the season. Duties include helping form teams, meeting with managers and coaches before the season, working through schedule issues, enforcing pitch count reporting, and helping administer post-season playoffs. Heaviest lift in late February and March; lighter admin work during the season.

### Summer Ball Coordinator
Organizes NCLL's sandlot-style summer league (late June–early August). Coordinates teams and ensures communication goes out to registrants and coaches.

### Umpire Coordinator
Schedules umpires for games — using volunteer umps first, professional if needed. Modest time commitment throughout the season. Strictly an admin role — no umpiring experience necessary. Umpire training responsibility lies with the Umpire-in-Chief.

### Jamboree Support
Assists the Jamboree Coordinator with organizing our annual mid-season classic — scheduling games, organizing concessions, etc. Busy in early season, then done after Jamboree.

## Background Checks

Per Little League International regulations, any person who interacts with kids during practices or games — even a team parent — must complete a background check. Once you register as a volunteer, you'll receive an email from our background check vendor (JDP) within 24 hours. The questionnaire takes only a few minutes and results typically return within 24 hours. **It is free and strictly confidential.**

## Corporate Volunteer Matching

Many companies (including Microsoft) will donate to NCLL as much as **$25 per hour** for your volunteer time. Check with your HR department to see if your company offers a matching program — it's one of the biggest drivers in keeping registration fees affordable year after year.
