Understanding Parts of a BB/SB Glove

  • Webbing: Closed webbing (pitchers, outfielders) hides the ball; open webbing (infielders) allows for quicker transfers.
  • Pockets: Shallow pockets for infielders, deeper pockets for outfielders.
  • Backs: Open backs (infielders) for flexibility; closed backs (outfielders) for support.
  • Wrists: Adjustable wrists for a secure fit.

Helpful Tips for Selecting the Right Glove

  • Consider the Position Played: Glove designs are often tailored for specific positions (e.g., infield gloves are smaller, while outfield gloves are larger to help catch fly balls).
  • Test the Glove's Fit: The glove should feel snug but not too tight. Make sure itâ€™s easy to open and close.
  • Break-In Period: New gloves can feel stiff at first, so encourage your player to wear them and practice catching regularly to break them in
  • Material Choices: Leather vs synthetic -- Higher quality and more durable but take longer to break-in vs.lightweight, affordable, and easier to break in but less durable.
  • Budget-Friendly Options: Many brands offer high-quality gloves at lower price points. Look for sales, especially during the offseason.

Top Baseball Glove Brands & Models for Kids

  • Rawlings Prodigy Series: Great for beginners and pre-teens. Lightweight and easy to close.
  • Wilson A200 Series: Affordable and designed for younger players with smaller hands
  • Mizuno Prospect Series: Known for having pre-oiled leather that requires minimal break-in

Top Softball Glove Brands & Models for Kids

  • Easton Ghost Series: A trusted choice for young softball players. Offers a softball-specific pocket design.
  • Wilson A500 Siren: Designed for beginner players; lightweight and comfortable
  • Mizuno Prospect Finch Series: Co-designed with Jennie Finch, ideal for youth players
