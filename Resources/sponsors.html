Meet Our 2026 Sponsors

North Central Little League runs on the generosity of local businesses who believe in giving kids a place to play, compete, and grow. This year we're proud to introduce the companies and neighbors who are making the 2026 season possible — as diverse as the neighborhoods we call home.

Phinney Ridge & Greenwood

The heart of NCLL country — and our deepest bench of supporters.

Bongos is a Caribbean playground tucked into a repurposed gas station on Aurora Ave N. The sandpit patio, jerk chicken, and rice bowls have made it a neighborhood staple since it opened. Stop in after a game and see why it's one of Seattle's most talked-about lunch spots.

56th Street Market has been the best kind of neighborhood store since 2011 — the kind that feels like it belongs. Open seven days a week at the corner of N 56th St, it stocks the staples you need plus a surprisingly deep selection of gluten-free and specialty goods, plus beer and wine delivery.

Ben's Bread Co. started as a sourdough pop-up and, after eight years, earned a storefront on N 70th St in the summer of 2023. Co-owners Ben and Megan Campbell — Ben is a James Beard semifinalist — turn out handmade loaves, pastries, and Wednesday night pizza using natural leavening and locally grown grains.

Phinney Books opened in June 2014 when eight-time Jeopardy! champion Tom Nissley invested his winnings in the neighborhood. At 7405 Greenwood Ave N, the carefully curated shelves cover fiction, kids' books, nature, Northwest titles, and more. Their "Phinney by Post" monthly book subscription is a local favorite.

Ken's Market has been the Greenwood and Phinney Ridge neighborhood grocery since 1955. Family-owned and community-invested, Ken's at 7231 Greenwood Ave N stocks pasture-raised meats, organic produce, free-range eggs, and all the everyday staples you'd expect — seven days a week, 7 am to 10 pm.

Focus Baseball is a Seattle-based nonprofit baseball academy operating out of 7806 Aurora Ave N. They run high-quality training programs for all ages and field competitive select teams at the 13U–15U level through the Perfect Game Baseball Association.

Kitch Switch is a design-build kitchen remodeling firm led by longtime partners Sheley (an award-winning designer-realtor) and Jon (30 years in construction management). They specialize in stylish, fast kitchen transformations — free consultations available at kitch-switch.com.

Greenwood Heating & Air has been keeping Seattle homes warm since Walter Zainey founded the Greenwood Furnace Co. right here in the neighborhood in 1918. More than a century later they still offer furnace service, heat pumps, mini-splits, and plumbing — and they still answer the phone at (206) 784-1818.

Green Lake

Fuller Living Construction was founded by Russell Fuller in 2010 and operates out of 5304 Green Lake Way N. Seattle's top-rated design-build remodeler on Google, they handle everything from kitchen and bath renovations to full home additions and commercial bar buildouts — ranked in the top 4% of all licensed Washington contractors.

Green Lake Orthodontics is Dr. Sara Cassidy's boutique practice on the shores of Green Lake at 7900 E Green Lake Drive N. A VIP Diamond Plus Invisalign provider, Dr. Sara treats patients of all ages with braces, Invisalign, and lingual braces in a welcoming office stocked with espresso and bubbly water.

Wallingford & Fremont

Murphy's Pub opened in 1981 at 1928 N 45th St, making it Seattle's oldest Irish pub. Owned by Phil and Chelley, it's a family and dog-friendly neighborhood anchor with live weekly music, classic pub fare (the Reuben is legendary), and a staff that feels like family.

Cedarhouse Flowers delivers lush, artful arrangements from their Wallingford shop at 1621 N 45th St. Nestled between Green Lake, Fremont, and the University District, they specialize in colorful everyday arrangements and modern, feminine wedding flowers.

Lighthouse Roasters has been roasting fine coffee in a 120-year-old craftsman building at 400 N 43rd St since 1993. Founder Ed Leebrick sources directly from dedicated farms and roasts to the sweet spot where body, flavor, and acidity find their balance. Notably wifi-free, it remains one of Seattle's great gathering spots.

Javila Creer — Windermere Real Estate is a Managing Broker and consistent top-1% Seattle agent based at Windermere Midtown, 1920 N 34th St. Licensed since 2003, Javila specializes in urban Seattle neighborhoods — exactly the ones NCLL calls home.

Ballard

Reuben's Brews opened in 2012 when homebrewer Adam Robbings named the taproom after his first child, Reuben. At 5010 14th Ave NW in the heart of the Ballard Brewery District, the award-winning brewpub pours 28 rotating taps alongside scratch-made pub food on a family and dog-friendly patio.

Harjo Construction is an award-winning design-build firm at 4900 15th Ave NW that has completed over 300 home renovation projects since 2010. Specializing in kitchen and bath remodels, basement conversions, additions, and ADUs, they bring a personal, client-first approach to every project.

North Seattle & Crown Hill

El Camion has been serving some of Seattle's most celebrated authentic Mexican food from its trucks and restaurant on Aurora Ave N since it earned rave reviews from The Seattle Times. The menu runs from tacos and burritos to gorditas, mulitas, tamales, and shrimp ceviche — plus a full catering operation for events.

Childish Things is North Seattle's favorite children's resale boutique, open since 2008 at 9776 Holman Road NW. The women-owned shop carries new and gently-used clothing, gear, shoes, and toys — including Jellycat, magnetic tiles, puzzles, and wooden toys — for kids and expecting parents alike.

Queen Anne & Interbay

Crystal Panes has been Seattle's go-to for residential and commercial window cleaning since 1985. Family-owned and operated out of 2212 Queen Anne Ave N, they offer interior and exterior window cleaning, gutter cleaning, and pressure washing — and have earned five consecutive Angie's List Super Service Awards.

Puffin Pediatrics is a community-focused, concierge-style pediatric clinic founded by Dr. Matthew Fradkin in 2024. Using a hybrid insurance and membership model, Puffin at 1631 15th Ave W keeps the practice small and accessible — open weekdays and Saturday mornings.

Wessco Blinds has been covering Seattle windows with custom blinds, shades, shutters, and motorized treatments for over 30 years. Their showroom is at 210 Nickerson St, and they offer free in-home estimates across the greater Seattle area.

Across Seattle

Dick's Drive-In opened its first location on January 28, 1954, in Wallingford and has been a Seattle institution ever since. Still family-owned, still Washington-only, and still serving hand-cut fries and made-to-order milkshakes at the same iconic stands that have defined fast food in the Pacific Northwest for over 70 years.

Tutta Bella was the Northwest's first certified Neapolitan pizzeria when it opened in 2004. Their pies are cooked for 90 seconds in 800° wood-fired ovens, and locations across Seattle and the Eastside have made them a go-to for families, date nights, and post-game celebrations alike.

Merlino Foods is a family-owned foodservice purveyor with roots going back to Angelo Merlino's 1900 arrival in Seattle from Italy. From their 98,000-square-foot SODO warehouse they supply hundreds of Seattle restaurants with everything from 22 varieties of mozzarella to specialty Italian imports.

Cutter & Buck was founded on Seattle's working waterfront in 1989, drawing its name from the sea (cutter) and the mountains (buck) that define the Pacific Northwest. Today their performance sportswear and golf apparel can be found in professional team stores and retailers worldwide — still headquartered right here in Seattle.

CubeSmart operates multiple self-storage facilities across Seattle, including a location at 9309 Aurora Ave N close to our fields. With climate-controlled units, drive-up access, and 24/7 video monitoring, they make it easy for families and businesses to store what they need.

Compass Construction is a Kirkland-based general contracting and project management firm with 27 years of experience across the Puget Sound. Specializing in residential and mixed-use construction, they bring 135 team members and an 80% repeat client rate to every project — a testament to the trust they've built over nearly three decades.

Foushée is one of the Puget Sound's leading commercial construction firms, operating since 1977. From office buildouts to retail renovation and LEED-certified projects, the Bellevue-based company has built a reputation for hard work, integrity, and keeping projects on time and on budget.

CONTINUUM Fitness & Strength, led by owner Zach Korte, offers individualized workout and nutrition plans built around a "Strength in simplicity" methodology. Based in Seattle, Continuum helps everyday athletes and fitness newcomers alike build lasting habits through clear, personalized programming — no gimmicks, just results. Zach has been a valued partner to NCLL for years, contributing to many league programs including helping launch the AAA/Majors Winterball training sessions.

Orca Analytics is a Seattle-based tech company building AI-powered growth tools for ecommerce brands. Their platform acts as a full analytics hub — consolidating data from Shopify, Meta, Google, and TikTok into one place so brands can optimize spend and scale profitably. Think of them as the analytics arm you wish every small business had.

Bear Mountain Capital is a Seattle-based independent wealth management firm led by CEO Joe Day, a Chartered Financial Analyst. Acting always as a fiduciary, they offer financial planning, portfolio management, and sustainable investing options for individuals, small businesses, and institutions.

Valley & Company Events has been designing spectacular weddings and events since 2003. Founded by husband-and-wife duo Aleah and Nick Valley, the firm has been named among the Top Planners in the World by Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, and Martha Stewart — and we're lucky to have them in our corner this season.

Seattle Police Department — our officers walk the beat, respond to calls, and keep our community safe every single day. We're proud to have SPD's support on and off the field.

Project 9 Brewing Co. is an award-winning, family- and dog-friendly brewery and tasting room at 1409 NE 80th St. They pour great craft beer in the mornings alongside Full Tank Coffee, and host Mexican Seoul for food — a neighborhood gem in Seattle's Maple Leaf/Roosevelt area.

Lincoln High School Baseball — Lincoln's Lynx compete in the WIAA and have built a strong program with a 9-3 record in 2024-25. The school's partnership with NCLL reflects a shared commitment to developing Seattle's next generation of ballplayers.

JustCoachNow connects young athletes with elite college-athlete coaches for private training. Their platform is built on the idea that access to high-quality instruction shouldn't be limited to a select few — a mission that aligns perfectly with NCLL's.

Seattle Saver Magazine helps Seattle-area families find local deals, discounts, and community resources. We appreciate their support in helping spread the word about North Central Little League.

Thank you to every sponsor, returning and new, who believes that a season of baseball and softball is worth investing in. NCLL is a community effort — and this list proves it.
