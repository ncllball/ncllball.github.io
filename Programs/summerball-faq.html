Frequently Asked Questions

Registration & Eligibility

Am I registering for the right Little League?

For Summerball, anyone is welcome — league boundary rules do not apply. Players from any neighborhood can join NCLL for the summer program.

What are the dates of the season?

Registration is open June 5—June 30. The season runs July 7 through August 13 (approximately five weeks, 10–12 games per team). See the events page for confirmed dates.

How much does it cost?

$75 per player. Show up for the weeks you can and vacation when you can't — there's no penalty for missing games. Scholarships are available by request prior to registration via the financial aid request form.

Note: Please do not purchase the registration insurance. It is a required option in the platform but is rarely useful. If you need to cancel, just contact us.

What to Expect

How often do we play?

No scheduled practices. Each team plays approximately two evening games per week — roughly 10–12 games total over five weeks.

What is “sandlot” format?

Sandlot divisions (Single-A Baseball, Softball) mean kids show up to the field and teams are formed from whoever is present and ready to play. There's no fixed team roster and no commitment to a specific group — families can come and go as their summer schedules allow.

Where do we play?

NCLL's home fields are Lower Woodland Park Ball Fields and Ross Park Fields. When playing interleague games, we travel to fields around the city — seldom more than a 30-minute drive. See the field locations and maps page for a full list.

What equipment and uniforms are provided?

A game jersey is included in the registration fee. Wear your spring season cap. Your player will need a baseball glove, a protective cup (recommended for boys), and footwear (cleats are not required — sneakers are fine). Personal bats and helmets are welcome if they meet Little League standards; shared equipment is available.

When will we know our team and schedule?

Team assignments go out approximately June 30–July 4 via Sports Connect email. The transition from the regular season to Summerball is fast — often managed by the same volunteers who just finished the regular season and All-Stars. We appreciate your patience!

More Questions?

Contact the Summerball coordinator at summerball@ncllball.com or the registrar at registrar@ncllball.com.
