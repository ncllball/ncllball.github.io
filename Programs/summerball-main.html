Summerball26 is NCLL’s summer baseball and softball program — open to any player, no geographic or school boundaries. No practices, no pressure. Just games with friends over six weeks in July and August. Flexible scheduling means no guilt when life gets busy; that goes for coaches too.

Registration is open June 6–30. Teams are sorted by friend requests and announced around July 4. Have questions? The Summerball FAQ has answers on divisions, schedules, fees, and more.

Summerball26 Program

Why Summerball26?

  • Flexible schedule = no guilt! (This goes for coaches too!)
  • No practices, just games!
  • Teams are sorted by friends! (Be sure to let us know who those friends are)
  • No geographic/school boundaries — ANYONE CAN PLAY!
  • Low-pressure, FUN baseball & softball!
  • Great & AFFORDABLE way to keep your player engaged in the game!

General Information

  • Format: Games only; no practices
  • Game Schedule: 2 games per week, total 10–12 games
  • Registration Fee: $75 per player (scholarships available)
  • Uniform: Game shirt provided; wear spring season cap
  • Required Equipment: Baseball glove, cup (boys), footwear
  • Optional Equipment: Personal bat and helmet (shared)

Summerball26 VOLUNTEERS

Summerball runs on parent volunteers — we need coaches and team managers for every division. We just need a small cadre to cover for each other — take your vacations, we all will. It’s summer.

During registration you’ll be asked if you’d like to volunteer — tap through for your options:

  • Lead Game Volunteer
  • Game Day Helper
  • Team Manager
  • Sandlot Team Organizer (A Baseball)

Sign up to volunteer during registration at the bottom of this page, then let your coordinator know when you arrive at your first game.

Lead Game Volunteer

  • Bring team gear, help with setup, ensure safety
  • Handle unusual situations on game day
  • Especially needed in Majors and Teen Baseball
  • Advanced players may coach bases (helmet required)

Game Day Helper

  • Dugout manager, base coach, umpire, or parent pitcher (AA)
  • Especially needed for AA/AAA Baseball and Softball Sandlot
  • AAA players may coach bases (with supervision)

Team Manager

  • Organize RSVPs, coordinate snacks, communicate with families
  • Great for non-coaching volunteers — no game experience needed

Sandlot Team Organizer (A Baseball)

  • Organize players, balance teams, keep it fun, oversee safety
  • Ideal for NCLL’s flexible “show up and play” format

Baseball AA/AAA/MAJ/JUN (Interleague)

Traditional organized baseball with set teams and regular game schedules. Competitive play against neighboring little leagues.

  • Games will be interleague play against neighboring leagues
  • Players are encouraged to play up to the level they’ll play next year
  • AA Baseball may have a hybrid mix of sandlot and interleague
  • Volunteer coaches needed — all are welcome to help!

Why Play Up in Summerball26?

  1. Better Competition — Local leagues (Ballard, Magnolia, Queen Anne, Shoreline, and more) have agreed to align divisions based on where players will be in Spring of ’27, creating balanced teams
  2. Low-Stakes, High-Growth — With All-Stars out playing tournaments, it’s the perfect time to safely adjust, take on new challenges, and build confidence in the new division
  3. Head Start for Spring 2027 — Getting early reps at the next level helps players settle in and succeed when the regular season begins
