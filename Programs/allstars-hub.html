All-Stars is NCLL's post-season tournament program, giving qualified players the chance to represent the league in District 8 competition and beyond. Teams are formed across multiple age brackets in both Baseball and Softball, with the possibility of advancing to State, Regionals, and — for some divisions — a Little League World Series.

Who can try out: Any player who has completed at least 60% of their regular-season games and meets the League Age requirement for a given bracket. Division played during the regular season does not determine eligibility. Download the application, complete it with a parent signature, and return it by May 14th at 9:00 PM.

Selection: Rosters are not decided at tryouts alone. Coaches evaluate players throughout the season — games, practices, attitude, and work ethic all factor in. The tryout on May 15th (Baseball) and May 17th (Softball) is a structured side-by-side evaluation, but it is one input among many. Rosters are announced Friday, May 22nd.

Questions? For Baseball contact Cole Parsons at baseball@ncllball.com. For Softball contact Gayle Perez at softball@ncllball.com. See the All-Stars FAQ for full details.

All-Stars Teams

10's

The 10's bracket is Baseball's youngest All-Star team, giving league-age 8–10 players their first taste of tournament competition.

  • Sport: Baseball
  • League Ages: 8–10
  • Tryout: May 15th — 6:00–7:00 PM, Lower Woodland Field #3/#4
  • Tournament: District 8 → State

Eligibility & What to Expect

Any player with League Age 8, 9, or 10 who has completed at least 60% of their regular-season games is eligible to try out. Playing up from a lower regular-season division is allowed — eligibility is based on League Age, not division.

The 10's tournament runs double-elimination format within District 8. Teams that advance compete at the State level. Practices begin June 1st; the tournament window runs late June through mid-July.

How to Apply

Download the All-Stars application, complete it, and return it by May 14th at 9:00 PM — hand it to your coach or scan and email it to baseball@ncllball.com. Rosters are announced May 22nd.

The 11's bracket is a Baseball All-Star team for league-age 9–11 players competing in District 8 and Washington State tournament play.

  • Sport: Baseball
  • League Ages: 9–11
  • Tryout: May 15th — 7:00–8:00 PM, Lower Woodland Field #3/#4

Players with League Age 9, 10, or 11 who have played at least 60% of their regular season are eligible. A League Age 10 playing on the 11's team is allowed but generally discouraged — coaches weigh team needs and fit alongside individual readiness.

The 11's tournament runs double-elimination at the District level, advancing to State. Practices begin June 1st with tournament play in late June through mid-July.

How to Apply

12's / Majors

The flagship Baseball All-Star bracket. The 12's/Majors team is the path to the Little League Baseball World Series in Williamsport.

  • Sport: Baseball
  • League Ages: 10–12
  • Tryout: May 15th — 8:00–9:00 PM, Lower Woodland Field #3/#4
  • Tournament: District 8 → State → Regionals → LLBWS

Eligibility & What to Expect

Players with League Age 10, 11, or 12 who have played at least 60% of their regular season are eligible. The 12's/Majors bracket is the path that leads to the Little League Baseball World Series — District 8 → Washington State Tournament → Western Regional (San Bernardino, CA) → Williamsport.

This bracket draws the most scrutiny at selection. Regular-season performance, attitude, work ethic, and the tryout all factor into roster decisions. Availability for the full tournament window is required.

How to Apply

Download the All-Stars application, complete it, and return it by May 14th at 9:00 PM — hand it to your coach or email baseball@ncllball.com. Rosters are announced May 22nd.

Juniors

Juniors All-Stars fields teams in both Baseball and Softball for league-age 13–14 players, competing on full-dimension fields at District 8 and beyond.

  • Sports: Baseball & Softball
  • League Ages: 13–14
  • BB Tryout: May 15th, Lower Woodland Field #3/#4
  • SB Tryout: May 17th, Lower Woodland (time TBA)
  • Tournament: District 8 → State → Regionals → World Series

Eligibility & What to Expect

Any league-age 13 or 14 player who has completed at least 60% of their regular season is eligible. In 2026, the Juniors division has a path to Regionals and a World Series — for both Baseball and Softball.

Juniors teams play on 90-foot bases. If NCLL doesn't have enough players to field a full independent team, the league may co-league with a neighboring league. Families are notified directly if that applies to this bracket.

How to Apply

Download the All-Stars application and return it by May 14th at 9:00 PM. Baseball: email baseball@ncllball.com. Softball: email softball@ncllball.com. Rosters announced May 22nd.

Seniors

Seniors All-Stars fields teams in both Baseball and Softball for league-age 15–16 players, running alongside high school ball on full-dimension fields.

  • Sports: Baseball & Softball
  • League Ages: 15–16
  • BB Tryout: May 15th, Lower Woodland Field #3/#4
  • SB Tryout: May 17th, Lower Woodland (time TBA)
  • Tournament: District 8 → State

Eligibility & What to Expect

Any league-age 15 or 16 player who has completed at least 60% of their regular season is eligible. Seniors co-leagues are common, particularly in Softball — families are notified directly if their age group will combine with a neighboring league.

Players competing in high school baseball or softball can manage both, but NCLL All-Star commitments take priority once a roster spot is accepted. Be upfront with both programs before the season begins.
