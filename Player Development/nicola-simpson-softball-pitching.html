Program Overview

We have a unique, last-minute winter pitching opportunity for AAA/Majors softball pitchers (returning or aspiring). Nicola Simpson will run a four-week, high-touch clinic at North Seattle Batting Cages. Two small-group Sunday blocks each week keep feedback tight, reps high, and mechanics ready for spring.

Four cohorts (3 pitchers each) meet for a fixed two-week stint at the same time each Sunday, so your pitcher and their group stay together for both dates.

New to club/select concepts? Read our primer on club/select/travel softball to understand how this training fits alongside NCLL.

This clinic is led independently by Nicola Simpson (not an NCLL program). Nicola coaches with Seattle Softball Academy and previously with Sting Fastpitch; she is bringing that club/select style of instruction to this small-group winter offering. Friends of NCLL event; NCLL scholarships are still honored.

About Coach Nicola Simpson

Accolades: WCWS third place (2023), SEC regular season and tournament champion (Tennessee), NFCA Division I Mountain Region Second Team (Pitcher/Utility, 2025), WA State Gatorade Player of the Year (2021), PGF All-American.

College: University of Tennessee (2021-2023), Seattle University (2024-2025) — led pitching staff in wins, ERA, complete games, and strikeouts; CSC Academic All-District; Easton/NFCA Scholar Athlete.

International: Canadian Senior National Team pitcher (Canada Cup, World Games) with a 1.31 ERA at the 2022 World Games; pitched against Team USA and Japan.

Prep: 498 career strikeouts, 14 no-hitters, 0.80 ERA, 3A state champion (2019), MaxPreps All-American, multiple academic honors.

Learn more on her Seattle University roster profile.

Skills & Focus

  • Pitch design, spin, and command tailored to AAA/Majors arms.
  • Power-line consistency, drive phase, and efficient arm circle.
  • Bullpen planning: warm-up, intent, and post-pen recovery routines.
  • Game awareness: sequencing, tempo, and working with catchers.
