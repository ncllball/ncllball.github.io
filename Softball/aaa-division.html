Welcome to the AAA (Minor) Division of our Little League softball program! As a Double Goal League, we emphasize both player development and competitive success. These guidelines supplement the Little League International ("Green Book") Softball Rules and highlight local points of emphasis for our league. When not otherwise specified below, official Little League Softball rules govern play.

This level is often a player's first full season of player pitch. The overarching goals are learning proper softball fundamentals, building confidence, encouraging participation in multiple defensive positions, and fostering love of the game while introducing situational awareness and strategy.

Pitching

Who Pitches

  • Any rostered player may pitch if they demonstrate safe mechanics in practice
  • Encourage multiple pitchers; development outweighs short‑term game outcome at this level

Pitching Distance

  • The pitching rubber is set at 35 feet from the back point of home plate (Little League Softball Minor / AAA standard)

Pitching Limits & Rest

  • Little League Softball uses innings pitched guidelines rather than pitch counts (unlike baseball)
  • Managers should monitor fatigue: loss of control, dropping elbow, slowing arm circle, or decreased velocity are signs to remove a pitcher
  • Rotate pitchers early in the season so more players gain circle experience before playoffs

Mechanics & Instruction

  • Stress a smooth arm circle, consistent stride, and safe follow‑through (avoiding abrupt shoulder recoil)
  • Focus on command (strikes) before movement pitches—no riseballs, drops, or curves are required at this level

Hit By Pitch

  • If a batter is hit by a legal pitch, they are awarded first base; encourage batters not to bail early and to turn away properly

Catcher / Pitcher Usage

  • To reduce overuse, avoid having the same player catch a full game and then pitch significant innings in the same day where possible
  • Warm‑up tosses between innings should be limited—use a backup player to start the inning if the pitcher is not ready

Illegal Pitch

  • Umpires will call obvious illegal pitches (crow hopping, replanting). Early in the season, most violations are handled as instructional warnings unless they create advantage

Hitting

Balls and Strikes

  • Standard softball count applies (4 balls / 3 strikes)
  • Foul balls with two strikes keep the batter alive (including tipped foul balls not securely caught)

Batting Order

  • Continuous (straight) lineup—every player bats in order, regardless of defensive participation that inning
  • Players arriving late are added to the bottom of the order without penalty
  • If a player cannot continue due to injury or illness, their spot is skipped (no automatic out)

Throwing Bats

  • One warning per team for an accidentally thrown bat; subsequent occurrences may result in the batter being declared out for safety

Offensive Coaching Focus

  • Prioritize timing, balanced stance, tracking the ball, and running hard out of the box
  • Bunting is optional—use it sparingly, focusing first on core swing development

Base Running

Leaving the Base

  • Runners may leave the base when the pitcher releases the ball (softball rule difference from baseball AAA)
  • Leaving early results in the runner being returned (warning) or called out after repeated violations

Stealing

  • Stealing of 2B and 3B is permitted once the ball is released
  • Advancing home on a passed ball / wild pitch / overthrow is permitted unless a local pre‑season notice states otherwise (Managers will be informed if a temporary restriction applies early in the year)
  • No delayed steal attempts prior to release—movement must begin after, not during the circle motion

Sliding

  • Sliding feet‑first is encouraged on close plays
  • Head‑first slides while advancing are not allowed; head‑first return to a base is permitted

Overthrows / Live Ball

  • Ball remains live on overthrows unless it enters dead‑ball territory (then standard base awards apply)
  • Teach runners to listen to base coaches and pick up the next base decisively

Base Coaches

  • Use both 1B and 3B coaches—consider trained parents if rostered staff is limited
  • Coaches should focus on clear, calm, early instructions ("hold", "round", "get down")

Fielding

Defensive Alignment

  • Standard 9 defensive players (P, C, 1B, 2B, SS, 3B, LF, CF, RF)
  • No 4th outfielder—focus on teaching traditional coverage responsibilities

Rotations & Minimum Play

  • Every player must have at least one infield and one outfield defensive opportunity during a regulation game when feasible
  • Strive to distribute innings (avoid one player locked at a single non‑battery position all game unless for safety)

Development Focus

  • Repetition on fundamental plays: force at 1B, throwing to correct base, backing up, communication on pop‑ups
  • Introduce cut‑off structure late season (SS for LF/CF, 2B for RF) but keep it simple—limit advanced relays early on

Umpire

Plate Umpire

  • The league assigns a plate umpire for regular season games whenever available
  • If absent at game time, the home team supplies a qualified adult; managers mutually agree if calls will be made from behind the plate or behind the pitcher (safety permitting)

Additional Umpire

  • During playoffs or interleague matchups, a second (base) umpire may be provided
  • The plate umpire has final authority on rules interpretations

Other Considerations

Ball Type

  • Official 11" Little League softballs are used (yellow cover, raised seams)

Bats

  • Must be Little League approved and display appropriate certification marks (e.g., USA/ASA)
  • Diameter typically 2 1/4"; discourage heavy bats that compromise swing mechanics

Inning Run Rule

  • 5‑run limit per half inning (stops when 5th run scores)
  • Final declared inning (or 6th) is open (no cap) to allow comeback opportunity—umpire & managers confirm before it starts

Game Length / Time

  • 6 innings or 2 hours (whichever comes first) — no new inning should start after the time limit reaches 1:50 unless both managers and umpire agree and daylight permits
  • Target efficient changeovers (2 minutes) to maximize innings played

Mercy Rule

  • 10‑run rule after 4 innings (3 1/2 if home team leads)
  • Managers may continue with an instructional scrimmage if both agree (official score ends)

Dugouts

  • Home team: 3rd base dugout; Visiting team: 1st base dugout
  • Keep equipment organized—bats secured, helmets forward, catcher's gear staged

Pre‑Game Warm Ups

  • Each team receives ~15 minutes of shared infield / outfield time if field conditions allow
  • If late due to prior game, managers agree on condensed format (e.g., infield only)

Equipment Checks

Gloves

  • Typical glove size 10"–12" for this age range (position dependent)
  • Label gear with player name to reduce lost & found volume

Helmets & Face Protection

  • Batting helmets must be free of cracks and have all padding present
  • Face masks on batting helmets are strongly recommended (and required in some interleague play)

Catcher's Gear

  • Set should include helmet w/ dangling throat guard, chest protector, leg guards, and catcher's mitt
  • Throat guard must remain attached; replace missing hardware promptly

Fielding Masks

  • Strongly recommended for all infielders; mandatory for pitchers where adopted by local policy

Interleague Play

  • AAA softball teams may schedule games with neighboring leagues (District 8)
  • Managers will clarify and agree on any local rule variances (run limits, stealing home, courtesy runners) during the plate meeting

End of Season Tournament

  • All teams participate in a season‑ending bracket for the division championship
  • Format (single or double elimination) and seeding details are communicated before tournament week

Final Thoughts

  • Development First: Prioritize skill growth, confidence, and enjoyment—wins follow consistent fundamentals
  • Positive Bench: Engage non‑active players (cheers, tracking lineup, gearing next catcher)
  • Clear Communication: Keep parents informed about rotation philosophy and safety emphasis
  • Respect & Safety: All regular on‑field participants must complete required volunteer clearances/background checks
