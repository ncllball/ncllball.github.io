Welcome to the AA Division of North Central Little League (NCLL) Softball (District 8 – Washington). This is an early player‑pitch transition division (coach pitch relief as needed). The emphasis remains development first while introducing player pitching, initial stealing concepts, and expanded defensive awareness.

These guidelines supplement the Little League Softball (Green Book) rules. When not specified below, official rules govern. Interleague games may require managers to clarify rule variance at the plate meeting.

Pitching

Distance

  • Pitching rubber at 35 ft (standard Little League Minor Softball distance)

Player vs Coach Pitch

  • Begin games with player pitchers when possible
  • If 3 consecutive walks or HBPs: optional switch to coach pitch to restore pace (finish the current batter scenario as agreed)
  • Coach pitch is underhand, firm, belt to mid‑thigh zone

Innings & Usage

  • No formal pitch count; monitor fatigue and mechanics (control loss, wrist drag, off‑balance follow‑through)
  • Encourage multiple pitchers—avoid overreliance on one player
  • Early season workload guidance lives in the shared Softball Pitching / Innings Reference

Mechanics Focus

  • Smooth arm circle, stable stride, full release extension
  • Avoid teaching spin pitches (drop, curve, rise) at this stage

Hit By Pitch

  • Award 1B; instruct players to turn away, not step forward

Catching

  • Rotate multiple players to build future depth
  • Full gear: helmet w/ throat guard, chest protector, leg guards, catcher’s mitt
  • Teach basic stance: athletic, glove as a target, block position introduction mid‑season

Hitting

  • Standard 4 balls / 3 strikes (foul w/ 2 strikes stays alive)
  • Thrown bat: team warning, repeat = batter out (safety)
  • Continuous lineup (straight batting order)
  • Late arrivals added to bottom of order

Base Running

Leaving Early

  • Runners may leave when the ball is released

Stealing

  • Early season: limit to 2B & 3B steals on release
  • Late season / interleague: confirm if stealing home allowed (varies)
  • No delayed steals during windup

Sliding

  • Feet‑first on advances; head‑first only when returning

Fielding

  • Standard 9 players; optional 10th (4th OF) in intra‑league play to increase reps
  • Introduce cutoffs: SS (LF/CF), 2B (RF) in second half
  • Minimum infield exposure: each player at least 1 infield inning when feasible

Game Rules

  • 5‑run per half inning limit (last declared or 6th open)
  • 90–105 minute target window (no new inning ~10 min before field end)
  • Home = 3rd base dugout / Visitor = 1st base dugout

Equipment

  • 11" softball (yellow, raised seam) standard
  • Typical gloves 10"–12"; pitchers discourage oversized trap pockets
  • Face masks encouraged on all batter helmets; defensive masks recommended for infielders

Final Thoughts

  • Development over outcome—especially early season
  • Share circle time and catching reps broadly
  • Reinforce softball IQ: force vs tag, when to run, which base to throw to
