NCLL Softball Pitching / Innings Reference

This page adapts Baseball pitch count structure into a Softball innings & workload frame. Little League Softball governs pitching by innings (not pitch counts) and emphasizes proper mechanics, rotation, and rest. Local guidelines below help manage developing arms and distribute opportunities—especially in April/May.

Rotation & Usage Principles

  • AAA/Majors: Intentionally schedule at least 2–3 pitchers weekly; avoid single-pitcher dependency.
  • Chart first-pitch strike % and walk rate to adjust bullpen focus.
  • Use short (1–2 inning) stints early to give more circle reps to developing pitchers.
  • Track cumulative innings per week; flag >8 (AAA/Majors) or >10 (Juniors/Seniors) for review.

Fatigue / Removal Signs

  • Arm recoil across body increases
  • Release point drifts (high / sailing pitches)
  • Loss of stride length or trunk posture collapse
  • Velocity drop accompanied by scattered misses
  • Player verbalizes forearm tightness (remove immediately)

Dual-Sport / External Load

  • Collect disclosure of club / select workload (innings & practice days)
  • Aim for at least 1 full calendar rest day from live pitching per week in April/May
  • Discourage back-to-back days except short closer-style appearance (≤1 inning)

Safety & Best Practices

  • Dynamic warm-up → wrist snaps → one-knee drill → full circle
  • Limit novelty pitches (rise / drop / curve) for pre-Majors; build fastball command and change-up deception first
  • Catchers: monitor receiving volume; rotate if catching every game + batting workload is high
  • Document innings in shared log (date, opponent, innings, notes)
