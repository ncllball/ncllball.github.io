Find the right division for your player

North Central Little League offers softball divisions for players ages 6-14, from first-time players learning the basics to competitive teams preparing for high school ball. Softball is designated for girls. Each division is designed to match players' developmental stages with appropriate skill challenges and game formats.

How to choose: Divisions are primarily based on your child's league age (their age on August 31st). Hover over any division card below to see full details about practice schedules, field locations, and what makes each division unique. If you're unsure which division is right, contact our registrar for guidance.

Registration for all divisions opens in early January. Assessments for AAA and Majors divisions typically occur in late February.

If you believe your player is capable of playing at a higher division than initially offered, indicate this during the registration process. This will be decided at assessments (AAA & Majors), so please sign up and plan on attending.

Note for coaches: If you need more detailed information than what's provided here — such as specific rules, draft procedures, or coaching resources — you're probably a coach! Head over to our Coaching section for comprehensive guides, training materials, and division-specific coaching information.

Softball Divisions

A - Coach Pitch

A Division introduces coach pitch with a softball, building fundamentals while keeping the game fun and accessible.

  • League ages: 6-7
  • Format: Coach pitch with 10'' safety ball
  • Weekly rhythm: 2 events per week — Tuesday and Saturday
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland
  • Pitching rubber: 35ft from home plate

Full A Division details

The A Division is primarily composed of 6 and 7-year-olds (league age) and serves as the entry point for softball players.

1. Introduction to Coach Pitch

  • A Division is coach pitch with a 10'' safety ball
  • Players learn fundamental softball skills in a supportive environment
  • Continuous batting order ensures everyone gets to hit

2. Team Selection Criteria

  • Coach/teammate requests
  • Siblings
  • School affiliations
  • Creating balanced teams within the division

3. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Practices will be 60-90 minutes until games begin
  • 2 events per week — Tuesday and Saturday

4. Practice Locations

  • A teams typically practice at either BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

AA - Coach/Player Pitch

AA blends coach pitch and player pitch so kids can compete while still learning situations and confidence on the mound.

  • League ages: 7-9
  • Format: Coach/player pitch mix with 11'' softball
  • Weekly rhythm: 2 events per week — Wednesday and Saturday
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland
  • Pitching rubber: 35ft from home plate

Full AA Division details

The AA Division is primarily composed of 7, 8 and 9-year-olds (league age) and serves as the next step for players graduating from A softball.

1. Player Age

  • The AA Division includes players who are primarily 7, 8 and 9 years old

2. Game Format

  • Games in AA are a combination of coach-pitch and player-pitch
  • Players continue learning softball rules and begin to understand the reasoning behind specific plays
  • Minimum play requirements ensure fair participation

3. Team Selection Criteria

  • Coach/teammate requests
  • Siblings
  • School affiliations
  • Creating parity within the division

4. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Practices will be 60-90 minutes until games begin
  • 2 events per week — Wednesday and Saturday

5. Practice Locations

  • AA teams typically practice at either BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

AAA

AAA is the launch point for full player pitch with assessments, parity drafts, and two games per week once the season begins.

  • League ages: 9-11
  • Format: Player pitch with 11'' softball
  • Weekly rhythm: 3 events per week — Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland
  • Pitching rubber: 35ft from home plate

Full AAA Division details

The AAA Division primarily consists of 9, 10 and 11-year-olds (league age) and serves as the next step for players graduating from AA softball.

1. Player Age

  • The AAA Division includes players who are primarily 9, 10 and 11 years old

2. Game Format

  • Games in AAA are player-pitch
  • This division balances instruction and competition, with a strong emphasis on player development
  • Minimum play requirements ensure fair participation

3. Assessments and Team Formation

  • Team formation is based on results from pre-season assessments
  • The goal is to create parity among teams
  • Players must participate in assessments to be considered for AAA softball

4. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Practices will be 60-90 minutes until games begin
  • 3 events per week — Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday

5. Practice Locations

  • AAA teams typically practice at either BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

Majors

Majors is the capstone for 10-12s with full evaluations, parity drafts, and a three-game weekly cadence.

  • League ages: 10-12
  • Format: Competitive player pitch with 12'' softball
  • Weekly rhythm: 3 events per week — Monday, Wednesday, Friday OR Saturday
  • Fields: BF Day or Lower Woodland
  • Pitching rubber: 40ft from home plate

Full Majors Division details

The Majors Division is composed of 10, 11 and 12-year-olds. It serves as the next step for players in this age group, especially those who excelled on previous AAA teams.

1. Player Evaluations

  • All players must attend player evaluations to be considered for Majors Softball

2. Instructional and Competitive

  • The Majors Division strikes a balance between instruction and competition
  • While still emphasizing player development, there is slightly more focus on the competitive aspects of the game compared to AAA
  • Minimum play requirements ensure fair participation

3. Manager Focus

  • Managers aim to field a competitive team while providing a fun learning experience for players
  • Players should concentrate on refining their softball skills, with consistency as a key goal

4. Team Formation

  • Team formation relies on pre-season assessments
  • The intention is to create parity among teams
  • Players must participate in assessments to be considered for Majors Softball

5. Practice and Game Schedule

  • Practices will be 60-90 minutes until games begin
  • 3 events per week — Monday, Wednesday, Friday OR Saturday

6. Practice Locations

  • Majors teams typically practice at either BF Day Field or Lower Woodland Fields

Junior Division

Junior softball moves to the full-sized diamond with interleague play across District 8.

  • League ages: 13-14
  • Format: 90-foot bases, district schedule, draft-based teams
  • Weekly rhythm: 3 events per week — Monday, Friday, Sunday
  • Fields: Greenlake or Lower Woodland #1
  • Pitching rubber: 43ft from home plate

Full Junior Division details

The Junior Division is specifically designed for players league age 13 and 14.

1. Field Size

  • Juniors play on a 90-foot diamond, which provides a more challenging and authentic softball experience

2. Minimum Play Requirements

  • Just like in other divisions, there are minimum play requirements to ensure fair participation for all players

3. Competition and District Play

  • North Central teams compete against other teams from our district
  • District 8 includes teams from Ballard, Queen Anne, Magnolia, Richmond, Northwest Seattle, and Northeast Seattle

4. Tryouts and Team Formation

  • Tryouts are held for player placement purposes
  • All players make a team, and team selection occurs through a draft process
  • The goal is to create parity among the teams

5. Practice Locations

  • Juniors teams typically practice at either Greenlake or Lower Woodland Field #1

Practice days will make every effort to match the days of the week games will be scheduled, however this is a goal, not a guarantee.

The scheduled days of softball events is listed under each division. This schedule was established long before our time and is agreed upon by all leagues in District 8. Although occasional tweaks are made, they are always in coordination with the district. For instance, if Majors play on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday at the district level, we must schedule our Majors games on those days. As a result, requests such as, "I would like to coach but can only make it on..." are nearly impossible to accommodate and largely out of our hands.

Please reach out to our VP of Softball for more information.
