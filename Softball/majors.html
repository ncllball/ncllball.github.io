Welcome to the Majors Division of NCLL Softball (District 8 – Washington). This is the highest standard Little League (league ages 11–12) division before moving to Juniors. We balance competitive play with player development and sustained love for the game. Unless modified below, Little League Official Softball Rules apply (Green Book).

Interleague play within District 8 is common. Managers must clarify any local rule adjustments (run limits, stealing home, courtesy runners) at the plate meeting.

Pitching

Distance

  • 40 ft rubber (Majors Softball standard)

Innings & Usage

  • No pitch counts; monitor fatigue & mechanics (arm circle shortening, loss of control, posture breakdown)
  • Encourage a rotation of at least 3–4 pitchers across the season

Mechanics & Development

  • Core focus: command & change of speeds (change‑up introduction)
  • Limit early pursuit of advanced spin pitches unless player demonstrates stable core mechanics

Illegal Pitch

  • Umpires enforce obvious replant / leap violations; early season: instructional warning unless advantage gained

Catcher Usage / Battery Management

  • Avoid full game catching + heavy circle workload in same day when possible
  • Warm‑ups: use backup to start half-inning when pitcher still prepping
  • See consolidated Softball Pitching / Innings Reference for early‑season ramp & workload philosophy

Hitting

  • Continuous lineup (straight batting)
  • Standard 4/3 count; foul with 2 strikes extends at‑bat
  • Thrown bat: one team warning then possible out for repeat
  • Offensive skill goals: timing, contact quality, situational awareness (RISP, advancing runners)

Base Running

Leaving the Base

  • Runners may leave on release

Stealing

  • Steals of any base permitted on release (confirm interleague stealing home allowances pre‑game)
  • Encourage aggressive but smart reads on passed balls / wild pitches

Sliding

  • Feet‑first on advances; head‑first only on returns
  • Teach hook and pop‑up slides for scoring plays later in season

Overthrows

  • Live ball unless dead‑ball territory entered (standard base awards)

Fielding

  • Standard 9 defensive players
  • Introduce / reinforce cut relay structure; communication on fly balls mandatory (priority: CF over corners, SS/2B over pitcher)
  • Minimum infield: strive for each player to have 1 infield inning over a two‑game rolling window (safety exceptions noted)

Game Rules / Structure

  • 6 innings regulation
  • 10‑run rule after 4 innings (3 1/2 if home ahead)
  • Run cap: 5 per half inning except final declared inning (open)
  • Time guideline: ~2:00; no new inning after 1:50 (local field slot dependent)
  • Dugouts: Home = 3rd; Visitor = 1st

Equipment

  • 12" Little League softballs (yellow, raised seams)
  • Bat certifications: current USA/ASA/USSSA where applicable (verify pre‑season guidance)
  • Face masks on batting helmets strongly encouraged / often required in interleague
  • Pitcher defensive masks recommended; some interleague opponents may require

End of Season Tournament

  • All teams seeded (method communicated pre‑tournament)
  • Bracket format (single / double elimination) published prior to start

Final Thoughts

  • Prioritize arm health (proper warm‑up, rest, mechanics)
  • Promote leadership: catchers & middle infielders drive defensive communication
  • Keep perspective—development & love of the game > standings
