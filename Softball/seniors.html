Welcome to the Seniors Softball Division (league ages 15–16) at North Central Little League (District 8 – Washington). This level most closely mirrors High School style play under the Little League structure while retaining community, inclusion, and developmental intent.

Unless modified for local application, Little League International (Green Book) rules govern. Interleague schedules across District 8 are standard—clarify any unique ground or local rule variations at the plate meeting.

Field Specifications

  • 60' base paths
  • 43' pitching distance
  • Fields rotate across District 8—confirm ground rules pre‑game

Competition Format

  • Full interleague schedule across District 8 leagues
  • Standings and seeding may be tracked (district dependent)
  • Post‑season District / All‑Star selection process communicated late spring

Team Formation

  • Tryout / evaluation based placement
  • All registered players placed; roster balance around pitching / catching depth emphasized

Game Rules

  • 7 innings regulation
  • 10‑run rule after 5 innings (4 1/2 if home ahead)
  • No new inning after local field/site time cap (typically ~2:00; confirm scheduling notice)

Pitching

  • Expanded repertoire acceptable (fastball, change‑up, movement pitches) with mechanics integrity
  • Monitor cumulative seasonal workload; promote recovery & shoulder care routines
  • Illegal pitches enforced; repeat infractions result in rule penalty per Green Book
  • See the unified Softball Pitching / Innings Reference for season ramp & safety guidelines

Hitting

  • Continuous lineup unless tournament format dictates otherwise
  • Strategic hitting layers: situational bunting, hit & run, backside approach vs velocity
  • Plate discipline tracked (quality at‑bats > pure outcomes)

Base Running

  • Aggressive leads on release; advanced secondary positioning taught
  • Delay steal & read timing integrated
  • Sliding diversity (hook, pop‑up, backdoor) situationally applied

Fielding

  • Advanced defensive communication (priority calls, coverage rotations, bunt defense layers)
  • Cut / relay structure refined; situational depth adjustments
  • Pitcher fielding practice (PFP): bunts, comebackers, force at home / 1B reads

Equipment

  • 12" Little League softballs (raised seam)
  • Approved bats must display current certification marks (USA/ASA/USSSA guidance)
  • Fielding masks strongly recommended for infielders (required in some interleague environments)
  • Full catcher's gear with dangling throat guard mandatory

Final Thoughts

  • Leadership & maturity emphasized—players own pre‑game routines & communication
  • Development remains central even in competitive contexts
  • Positive culture and sportsmanship define program identity
