Welcome to the A Division of North Central Little League (NCLL) Softball (District 8 – Washington). This machine / coach pitch instructional level focuses on player development, confidence, safety, and fun. These local guidelines supplement Little League Softball rules (Green Book). Where not explicitly covered here, official Little League Softball rules govern.

This page also lists District 8 interleague / local rule resources used for older divisions so coaches begin to familiarize themselves with the pathway upward.

Pitching & Catching (Machine / Coach Pitch)

Offensive team coach (or approved volunteer) operates a pitching machine (if used) or delivers soft coach pitches. The objective is hittable, confidence‑building pitches—not strikeout focus.

Pitching Machine Operation

  • Coach/parent from batting team operates the machine
  • Adjust speed / arc to create consistent belt‑high pitches
  • If machine malfunctions, switch to soft coach pitch

Placement

  • Machine (or coach) positioned approximately 35 ft (simulating later player‑pitch distance of 35–40 ft)
  • Coaches beginning to introduce future awareness: see Softball Pitching / Innings Reference for how player pitching ramps later

Catching

  • Catcher (optional early season) encouraged by mid‑season for throwing / receiving development
  • Helmet w/ throat guard, chest protector, leg guards always required when a player is in the catching position

Hitting

  • No walks recorded—goal is to put the ball in play
  • Foul balls extend the at‑bat
  • Consider a 4 or 5 pitch (or swing) maximum then coach soft toss if needed
  • Thrown bat: one warning; repeat -> sit for the half‑inning (safety)

Base Running

  • Emphasize quick start out of the box and listening to base coach
  • No stealing or leading off (development focus)
  • Advance one base on most overthrows to maintain pace / teaching rhythm
  • Teach force play awareness and simple “freeze on line drive” concept late season

Fielding

  • Rotate players—no position specialization (battery exceptions only for safety)
  • OK to use 4 outfielders to maximize participation
  • Focus: athletic ready position, two‑handed catches, correct direction on grounders (forehand/backhand)

Game Structure

  • Target 3–4 innings inside time window (75–80 minutes typical)
  • 5‑run or full lineup bat around guideline (agree pre‑game)
  • No official score kept; celebrate development moments

Equipment

  • Use 10" or RIF (safety) softball per league equipment assignment
  • Typical glove size 9"–11"; discourage oversized traps
  • Facemasks on batting helmets strongly encouraged early to normalize safety
  • Infield defensive masks optional but supported—especially 1B & pitcher spots

Bench / Parent Involvement

  • Engage parents as base coaches, dugout helpers (background check required)
  • Keep bench organized: next 3 hitters staged w/ helmets
  • Positive reinforcement over outcome commentary (“Great ready position!” vs “Throw it sooner!”)

Final Thoughts

  • Create a fun, encouraging learning environment
  • Model sportsmanship & volunteer spirit
  • Prepare athletes for AA softball fundamentals (throwing progression, baserunning awareness, receiving)
