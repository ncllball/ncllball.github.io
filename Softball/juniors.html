Welcome to the Juniors Softball Division (league ages 13–14) at North Central Little League (District 8 – Washington). This level bridges Majors and higher‑level competitive play (including future High School). We emphasize refinement of fundamentals, expanded strategy, and continued character and team culture growth.

Unless modified here for local emphasis, Little League International (Green Book) Softball rules govern. Interleague and District scheduling is common—clarify local adjustments (run rules, time limits) at the plate meeting.

Field Specifications

  • 60' base paths
  • 43' pitching distance (Juniors Softball standard)
  • Games scheduled across District 8 fields; confirm ground rules pre‑game

Competition Format

  • Interleague schedule vs neighboring District 8 leagues
  • Standings may be maintained (district dependent)
  • Post‑season opportunities (District / All‑Stars) communicated mid‑season

Team Formation

  • Players evaluated (tryout style) pre‑season
  • Formation targets balance of pitching & catching distribution
  • All registered players placed

Pitching

  • 43' distance introduces need for deeper command & change‑up sophistication
  • Monitor mechanics for overuse: posture collapse, shoulder recoil, reduced hip drive
  • Encourage development of a change‑up; defer rise/drop/curve until consistency
  • Illegal pitches (replant / leap) enforced—early season corrective instruction encouraged
  • Reference the league‑wide Softball Pitching / Innings Reference for early‑season workload ramp & rotation strategy

Hitting

  • Continuous lineup (unless tournament rules specify otherwise)
  • Offensive approach: timing first, zone recognition, situational execution (sac, hit & run optional)
  • Bunting, slapping, and power approaches can be layered as appropriate

Base Running

  • Leave on release; aggressive reads on passed balls encouraged
  • Introduce delay steal timing and pitcher rhythm recognition
  • Sliding variety (hook, pop‑up, backdoor) taught progressively

Fielding

  • Advanced cut & relay organization (SS/2B priorities)
  • Outfield communication & drop step technique emphasized
  • Corner infield: bunt defense & crash communication added

Game Structure

  • 7 innings regulation
  • 10‑run rule after 5 innings (4 1/2 if home leads)
  • Time guideline ~2:00 (district / light dependent)

Equipment

  • 12" Little League softballs (raised seam)
  • Bat certification: USA/ASA/USSSA marks per Little League guidance
  • Infield defensive masks strongly recommended (pitcher, 3B especially)
  • Full catcher's gear: dangling throat guard required

Final Thoughts

  • Promote leadership roles—catchers & middle infielders drive defense
  • Arm care: dynamic warm‑ups, proper recovery emphasis
  • Pathway readiness: prepare players for potential High School tryouts
