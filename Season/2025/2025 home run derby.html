On a beautiful May evening at the Cloverleaf at Lower Woodland Park, NCLL held its annual Home Run Derby — and the community showed up in force. Softball Majors players took the plate first, then Baseball Majors contestants stepped up, Seattle Pops kept things sweet, and a piece of NCLL history was made when a Lincoln High School student threw out the very first ceremonial first pitch. Congratulations to all of our contestants, and to our 2025 champions.

Softball

Winner: Nora Dodgion — congratulations to Nora on claiming the title for the third consecutive year! Nora hit at least 54 home runs, an extraordinary performance that put the field well out of reach — Ruby Way came in second with 9. With this being Nora's final year of Little League eligibility, she went out swinging — and the crowd knew it.

2025 Softball Home Run Derby contestants.

Baseball

Winner: Luke Walbridge — well done, Luke! The baseball derby came down to a swing-off tiebreaker, with Luke edging the field with 17 home runs to claim the title and his Tsunami Bat prize.

Jasper Shikella, Beckett McGinnis, Alexander Mikolajczak, Tyger Austin, Blake McIntyre, James Donovan, Beckett Loy, Owen Parsons, Nick Rolfe, John Arnette, Vinnie Dean.

A Historic First Pitch

Before a single bat was swung, a Lincoln High School junior stepped to the mound. Riko Hibino — who started a Change.org petition calling for turfed softball fields in Seattle — threw out the ceremonial first pitch, with 1,111 signatures behind her cause and a Seattle Times front-page story ("Seattle has a softball field problem") bringing her fight into the open. The turfed fields issue affects NCLL players, local high school programs, and adult rec leagues alike. We were proud to give Riko the spotlight, and we hope every NCLL family added their name to her petition.
