2026 NCLL Home Run Derby – Recap

On a beautiful May evening at Lower Woodland Park, NCLL held its annual Home Run Derby — and this year delivered some great moments. Softball and Baseball Majors players stepped up to the plate, the Dick's Drive-In food truck kept the crowd fed, and a moment of NCLL history was made when a mom took the mound for the very first time.

Softball

Winner: Izzi Parsons — congratulations to Izzi on taking the title! Nora Dodgion, who held the crown for three consecutive years, is a tough act to follow.

Contestants (L→R): Nora Dodgion, Elisa Pérez, Avery Baker, Ruby Way, Izzi Parsons. Not pictured: Noe Smith, Cami Richardson.

Baseball

Winner: Luke Jacobsen — well done, Luke!

Back row (L→R): Auggie Hilen, Harlan Putman, Quinault Fuller, Will Millea, Alex Dallmeyer, Elliot York-Jones, Austin Christofferson, Luke Jacobsen, Henry Tufte. Front row: Brooks Bressler, Lucas Wickland. Not pictured: Kai Johnston.

A Historic First

In the history of the NCLL Home Run Derby, the pitcher's role has always gone to a coach or a father. This year, Emma York-Jones changed that — becoming the first mother ever to pitch in the baseball derby, throwing for her son Elliot York-Jones. We love this.

Champions

Softball winner Izzi Parsons and baseball winner Luke Jacobsen.
