Managers and Coaches

NCLL relies heavily on its managers and coaches. This section provides an overview of the information and resources available to them. Despite the perception that coaching baseball or softball requires prior experience, many coaches in our older divisions (AAA & Major) began their coaching journey at the Tee-Ball level with no prior experience.

There is a discrepancy between the LLI nomenclature and what resonates with our families, particularly between 'Team Manager' and 'Head Coach'. Using 'Team Manager' for a head coach confuses families and diminishes the role our parents understand as 'Team Manager' (team parent role). Therefore, for 2025, we've updated the terms to:

  • Head Coach
  • Assistant Coach
  • Team Manager

I wanted to clarify these terms to avoid confusion for those accustomed to the previous terminology. For 2025, these are the terms NCLL will be using.

Head Coach Responsibilities

  • Player Evaluation & Selection: Attends tryouts and evaluates player performance to draft the team roster.
  • Practice Coordination: Designs and implements practice plans with input and assistance from Assistant Coaches.
  • Game Strategy & Lineups: Establishes game positions, lineups, and strategies while ensuring compliance with the Little League Greenbook Rules and STLL By-laws, particularly regarding equitable playing time.
  • Clear Communication: Maintains open and effective communication with players, parents, and league officials.
  • Building Team Spirit: Promotes a positive, inclusive, and supportive team environment.
  • Volunteer Organization: Works with the Team Parent to ensure volunteer roles, including umpire assignments, are covered for every game.
  • League Commitment: Attends all required meetings and trainings, such as Safety and Division Meetings, Coaches Clinics, and Umpire Training sessions.

Assistant Coach Responsibilities

  • Practice Assistance: Carries out the practice plans set by the Head Coach and actively participates in drills to enhance player development.
  • Game Day Duties: Serves in game-day roles such as base coach or dugout coach, as directed by the Head Coach.
  • Support & Leadership: Provides constructive feedback to the Head Coach and takes charge of the team when the Head Coach is unavailable.

Team Manager Responsibilities

  • Game Support: Assists with pitch counts, scoring, or other in-game administrative tasks as needed.
  • Dugout Coordination: Helps manage the dugout to keep things running smoothly.
  • Uniform Distribution: Ensures all players receive their jerseys and gear.
  • Paperwork Management: Collects and organizes all necessary team paperwork.
  • End-of-Season Celebration: Plans and coordinates a fun team party to wrap up the season.

Conduct

NCLL is a Positive Coaching league. All managers must attend a seminar from the Positive Coaching Alliance (PCA) and uphold the rules of conduct set forth by NCLL and PCA. NCLL believes that baseball and softball are the means to helping kids learn and grow, not just as athletes, but as people.

Registration

  • Complete the volunteer registration through the NCLL website. (This applies to all coaches, including assistant coaches and team managers.)
  • If you are a parent of a player who indicated interest in coaching during the player registration process, you must still complete the coach registration.
  • Once NCLL has your information, they will ask the background check vendor, JDP, to send you a questionnaire. For more information on background checks, visit the background checks page.
  • If you do not intend to return to coaching, please inform the Registrar].

Team Formation & Manager Assignments

  • New managers should indicate their interest in leading a team during the registration process. Teams will be formed around the manager and their child, taking into account placement requests as much as possible.
  • Returning managers should also indicate their interest in managing. All efforts will be made to keep players and teams together from previous years at the AA level and below. Players returning from the previous season should register before the deadline to guarantee a spot on the same team. Assistant coaches can request teams at AA and below.
  • At the AAA level and above, teams are formed using a draft system. This is done to achieve parity, a requirement of Little League International. All players interested in playing at these levels will participate in a formal assessment. For more information, visit the Assessments page.

Coach's Meeting

In late February, all coaches will gather to learn about the upcoming season and hear from experienced coaches about team management, organizing practices, and motivation.
