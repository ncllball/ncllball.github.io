Positive Coaching

NCLL is a double-goal league, which means its managers and coaches are trained yearly by the Positive Coaching Alliance (PCA). PCA promotes an approach to youth sports that focuses on:

  • Developing the whole athlete
  • Using sports to teach life lessons
  • Supporting coaches and parents to think beyond wins and losses

Several organizations offer valuable resources on coaching the whole person, inspiring risk-taking, and encouraging a growth mindset in players. One example is Positive Coaching Alliance who:

  • Provides resources for coaches, parents, and athletes
  • Offers weekly email reminders during the season
  • Includes videos, research, and information to guide coaching practice in all sports

Parent/Guardian Pre-season Meeting

Why have a pre-season parent meeting?

  • People tend to live up to expectations if they know them.
  • A meeting can help mold the behavior of your athletes' parents.
  • It's a chance to set the tone for the season and communicate important information.

When and where to have the meeting:

  • The most effective way to have your pre-season parent meeting is in a private setting where you can have the full attention of the group.
  • If this is not possible, the meeting could be scheduled before one of the first practices or games when the parents would need to be delivering their kids anyway.

What to cover in the meeting:

  • Introductions: Introduce yourself and any other coaches or team officials.
  • Team goals: Discuss the team's goals for the season, both on and off the field.
  • Expectations: Outline your expectations for the players and parents, including behavior, attendance, and communication.
  • Schedule: Review the team's practice and game schedule.
  • Communication: Discuss how you will communicate with parents throughout the season.
  • Questions and answers: Allow time for parents to ask questions and share their concerns.
