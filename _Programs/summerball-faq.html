FAQs

Q: What division should my player register for?

A: The best question to ask: what division will my player be in next spring? — then there is your answer! Playing up a level is encouraged for older or more experienced players. See the Division Guidance card on the main Summerball page for a full breakdown.

Q: What are the dates of the season?

A: Registration is open June 6–June 30. The season runs July 6 through August 14 (approximately six weeks, 10–12 games per team). Team assignments go out around July 4 via Sports Connect email.

Q: How much does it cost?

A: $75 per player. Show up for the weeks you can and vacation when you can't — there's no penalty for missing games. Financial assistance is available by request prior to registration via the financial aid request form .

Q: Am I registering for the right Little League?

A: For Summerball, anyone is welcome — league boundary rules do not apply. Players from any neighborhood can join NCLL for the summer program. If you played spring ball with another league, you're still eligible to register with NCLL for Summerball.

Q: What is “sandlot” format?

A: Sandlot divisions (Baseball A, Softball A/AA, and Softball AAA/Majors) mean kids show up to the field and teams are formed from whoever is present and ready to play. There's no fixed team roster and no commitment to a specific group — families can come and go as their summer schedules allow.

Q: What is “interleague”?

A: Interleague divisions (Baseball AA through Juniors) play scheduled games against teams from other Seattle-area Little League programs. Your player will have a fixed roster and play roughly half their games at home fields and half away — seldom more than a 30-minute drive.

Q: Can my player play up or down a division?

A: Playing up is encouraged for older or more experienced players — Summerball is a great low-stakes environment to take on a new challenge. Playing down is less common but can be arranged. Contact summerball@ncllball.com before or after registration to discuss.

Q: Can I request to be on the same team as a friend?

A: Absolutely — friend requests are our number one rostering criteria for interleague teams. List your requests at registration and we’ll do our best to honor them. Sandlot divisions are more loosely formed — teams are picked on game day from whoever shows up, so there’s no pre-set roster.

Q: How often do we play?

A: No scheduled practices. Each team plays approximately two evening games per week — roughly 10–12 games total over six weeks.

A: NCLL’s home baseball fields are Lower Woodland Park and Ross Park. Softball plays at Shoreline or Ballard — co-league and final field details TBD. Q: What equipment and uniforms are provided?

A: A game jersey is included in the registration fee. Wear your spring season cap or any cap. Your player will need a glove, a protective cup (required for baseball), and footwear (cleats are not required — sneakers are fine). Personal bats and helmets are welcome if they meet Little League standards; shared equipment is available.

Q: What if we miss games?

A: No attendance requirement — come when you can, skip when you can't. That flexibility is part of what makes Summerball work. For interleague divisions, give your team manager or lead game volunteer a heads-up when you know you’ll be out. Sandlot divisions are pick-up format — no roster, no check-in needed.

Q: What happens if a game is rained out?

A: Cancellations are announced via GameChanger as early as possible — usually by late afternoon on game day. We make every effort to reschedule rained-out games, but not all can be made up within the season window. Check GameChanger for the latest updates.

Q: When will we know our team and schedule?

A: Team assignments go out around July 4 via Sports Connect email. The transition from the regular season to Summerball is fast — often managed by the same volunteers who just finished the regular season and All-Stars. We appreciate your patience!

Q: How do I volunteer?

A: You can indicate your interest when registering and select a role at that time. We’re especially looking for Lead Game Volunteers, Game Day Helpers, Team Managers, and Sandlot Team Organizers. Even if you can only help with a few games, every bit counts. See the Volunteer Hub for details — including a step-by-step sign-up guide if your player isn’t yet registered for the summer.

Q: Who do I contact with questions?

A: The Contacts card on the Summerball26 page has a full list of who handles what — each coordinator has a specific lane (baseball, softball, Baseball A, registration). For general questions, reach summerball@ncllball.com .
